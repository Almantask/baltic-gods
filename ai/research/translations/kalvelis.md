# Kalvelis / Kalvis / Debesu kalējs

## Comprehensive Overview

"Kalvelis" (Little Smith) and "Kalvis" (The Smith) are names for the "Celestial Smith" in Baltic mythology. He is the creator of the celestial bodies and the weapons of the gods, acting as a divine artisan whose work maintains the cosmic order.

### English (EN)
**Kalvelis** (often identify with or as a variant of **Teliavelis**) is the divine smith who forged the Sun (**Saulė**) and placed it in the heavens. In the Baltic creation myth fragment, long after the world was created, the Sun was stolen/hidden by dark spirits, and it was Kalvelis who hammered its golden brilliance and tossed it back into the sky. He is also the one who forged the axe and hammer of **Perkūnas**, allowing the Thunderer to strike down evil forces. In Latvian mythology, he is the **Debesu kalējs** (Celestial Smith), often heard hammering during thunderless summer evenings (heat lightning). He is a figure of technical mastery and generative power, responsible for "shaping" the world's most vital elements.

### Lithuanian (LT)
**Kalvelis** (arba **Kalvis**) lietuvių mitologijoje yra dieviškasis amatininkas, dangaus kalvis. Jo svarbiausias darbas — nukalti Saulę ir įmesti ją į dangų, kad ši šviestų žmonėms. Jis dažnai tapatinamas su **Teliaveliu**, kurį mini Ipatijaus metraštis (1261 m.). Kaip Perkūno pagalbininkas, Kalvelis kala jam žaibus, kirvius ir karius ginklus. Jis įkūnija archajišką kalvystės sakralumą; kalvė senovės lietuvių pasaulėžiūroje buvo vieta, kur žemiškoji ugnis susijungia su dieviškąja kūryba. Tautosakoje sakoma, kad vakaro žara ar tolimas griausmas be lietaus yra Kalvelio darbo ženklas.

### Latvian (LV)
**Debesu kalējs** latviešu dainās ir Dieva dēlu vai paša Dieva palīgs, kurš darina saules saktu, zvaigžņu jostas un Pērkona ieročus. Viņa kalve atrodas kalnā vai debesu malā, kur dzirksteles lido kā zvaigznes. Kalējs personificē ne tikai fizisku darbu, bet arī kosmiskās kārtības atjaunošanu. Bieži dzirdētais teiciens par „saules kalšanu” norāda uz gaismas atgriešanos un dabas atmošanos. Viņa darbs ir svēts, un kalēja arods senlatviešu sabiedrībā tika godāts līdzīgi viedajiem un priesteriem.

## Primary Source Details

- **Hypatian Chronicle (Hyp):** Mentions *Teliavelis* as the smith who forged the sun. [1261 entry]
- **Vėlius (Vel):** Analyzes the role of the celestial smith as a mediator between the sky and the earth, often involved in the restoration of the sun. [Norbertas Vėlius]
- **VLE:** Describes Kalvelis as the mythic artisan who forged the celestial bodies and divine weapons.
- **Folklore (Bas):** Records legends of a "giant smith" who could hammer mountains and create the stars.

## Key Metadata
- **Region**: Lithuania, Latvia, Prussia
- **Tribe**: All Baltic tribes
- **Period**: Proto-Baltic origin
- **Category**: Celestial / Craft Deity
- **Symbol**: Anvil, Hammer, Golden Sun, Sparks (Stars)
- **Animals**: Horse (steed of the smith)
- **Function**: Forging the Sun, Creating divine weapons, Technical skill, Cosmic order
- **Etymology**: *kal- (to hammer/strike)
