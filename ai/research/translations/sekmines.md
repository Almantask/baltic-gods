# Sekminės — Lithuanian Pentecost / Spring-Greening Festival

## Comprehensive Overview

**Sekminės** is the Lithuanian Pentecost festival, observed fifty days after Easter (late May to early June). While aligned with the Christian feast of the Holy Spirit, Sekminės draws heavily on an older pre-Christian spring-greening tradition centred on birch trees, fresh greenery, and the invocation of spring fertility before the solstice. The name derives from *sekminis* (seventh), as Pentecost falls on the seventh Sunday after Easter.

### English (EN)
**Sekminės** is the Lithuanian late-spring festival coinciding with Christian Pentecost. Its dominant pre-Christian feature is the decoration of homes, doorways, farmsteads, and wells with freshly cut **birch branches** (*beržai*). Birch boughs are placed on window ledges, gates, stable doors, and along pathways — the fragrant, newly-leaved branches are seen as a conduit for spring's life-force and a protective barrier against summer disease. Young birches are brought into the house and sometimes adorned with ribbons. Girls weave garlands of the first summer flowers. Community processions wind through the village carrying birch branches, singing songs of the green world in bloom. The spiritual patrons are forest, field, and meadow deities: **Medeina / Medinė** (Forest Goddess), **Kaupolis/Kupolė** (flowering and vegetation), and **Žemyna** (Earth). The birch tree holds particular sacred status in Lithuanian and Baltic tradition as the World Tree's earthly manifestation — young, graceful, and luminous with new life.

### Lithuanian (LT)
**Sekminės** – švenčiamos 50 d. po Velykų, tačiau su galingu ikikrikščionišku pagrindu. Tradiciškai Sekminių rytą langai, vartai ir tvartai papuošiami šviežiomis berželių šakutėmis. Berželis – ypatingas lietuviams medis, simbolizuojantis švarumą, jaunystę ir gamtinę gyvybę. Giedamos dainos vasaros gamtai, merginų pynami vainikai iš pirmų vasaros žiedų. Šventės globėjai – **Medeina**, **Kupolė** ir **Žemyna**. Sekminės pažymi paskutinę didžiąją pavasario šventę iki vasaros saulėgrįžos.

### Latvian (LV)
**Vasaras svētku** priekšvakarā latviešu tradīcijā notiek bērzu un zāļu vešana mājās — tieša paralēle lietuviešu Sekminių tradīcijai. Abi tradīcijas saglabā pavasara rituālu struktūru, kurā svaigi bērzu žagari un ziedu vainagi nostiprina māju pret slimībām un nodrošina ganāmpulka auglību vasaras laikā.

## Key Metadata
- **Region**: Lithuania-wide
- **Tribe**: Lithuanians, Samogitians
- **Period**: Pre-Christian origin; aligned with Christian Pentecost from 15th century
- **Category**: Calendar Festival / Spring-Greening Ritual
- **Date**: 50 days after Easter (moveable, late May – early June)
- **Symbol**: Birch branch (*beržas*), Flower garland, Green pathway
- **Deities invoked**: Medeina (forest), Kupolė/Kaupolis (vegetation), Žemyna (earth)
- **Ritual actions**: Birch-branch decoration, processions, flower garland weaving, spring songs
