# Kaladų vakaras — Lithuanian Winter Solstice Log-Burning Eve

## Comprehensive Overview

**Kaladų vakaras** ("Log Evening") is the Lithuanian winter solstice eve ceremony centred on the burning of a large ceremonial log (*kaladą* or *kaladė*). It marks the astronomical longest night, the death of the old solar year, and the rebirth of the sun. The ceremony correlates with pan-European Yule log traditions but has specifically Baltic features tied to **Gabija** (the sacred fire), **Dievas** (the sky father), and ancestral spirit veneration.

### English (EN)
**Kaladų vakaras** is the Lithuanian winter solstice eve, celebrated around December 21–23. The central act is dragging a large log (*kaladė*) from the forest into the farmstead and burning it in the hearth or courtyard fire. The log must burn throughout the entire solstice night — its sustained burning magically ensures the sun's return. If the log burns down completely and cleanly, the coming year will be good; if it smoulders badly or goes out, misfortune is predicted. Ash from the Kaladų log is preserved: sprinkled on fields in spring, it protects crops from frost and vermin. Offerings of beer, bread, and salt are made to the fire as the log is lit; **Dievas** (the sky father/cosmic order) is invoked to resume his celestial governance of the year. Family members circle the fire, singing songs of the returning sun. The eldest woman of the household addresses **Gabija** and asks her to keep the family safe through the darkest night. The event is a domestic parallel to the great communal bonfire traditions of the summer solstice, transposed into the indoor hearth-fire in winter.

### Lithuanian (LT)
**Kaladų vakaras** – žiemos saulėgrįžos (apie gruodžio 21–23 d.) šventė, kurios centras – didelės birzgės (*kaladė*) deginimas. Birzgė turi degti visą ilgiausią naktį: jei dega gerai – metai bus sėkmingi; jei užgęsta – blogai. Jos pelenai saugomi iki pavasario ir beriami ant laukų. Prie laužo arba židinio aukojamas alus, duona ir druska. Dainuojamos dainos, šlovinančios grįžtančią saulę. Šeimininkė kalba **Gabijai** ir prašo saugoti namus per tamsiąją naktį. Pranė Dundulienė (*Dund*) dokumentuoja kaladų tradicijos simbolinę struktūrą – birzgė kaip seno metų mirties ir naujo gimimo simbolis.

### Latvian (LV)
**Bluķa vakars** (latviešu paralēle) ir identiska tradīcija — latviešu Bluķis (Ziemassvētku rēķis) tika vilkts apkārt mājai un sētai pirms sadedzināšanas, lai padzītu ļaunos garus. Abās tradīcijās — lietuviešu un latviešu — koka baļķis ir ziemas ļaunuma personifikācija, ko rituāla uguns iznīcina, tādā veidā sagatavojot ceļu jaunajai saulei. Šī paralēle ir viens no spilgtākajiem kopīgā baltu rituālā mantojuma pierādījumiem.

## Key Metadata
- **Region**: Lithuania-wide (especially Aukštaitija and Dzūkija)
- **Tribe**: Lithuanians
- **Period**: Pre-Christian; attested in folk custom; parallel to pan-European Yule traditions
- **Category**: Calendar Festival / Solar Rebirth / Fire Ritual
- **Date**: December 21–23 (Winter Solstice eve)
- **Symbol**: Ceremonial log (*kaladė*), Hearth fire, Preserved ash, Beer offering
- **Deities invoked**: Gabija (hearth fire), Dievas (cosmic renewal), ancestral Vėlės
- **Ritual actions**: Log-dragging, all-night burning, beer/bread offering, sun-return songs, ash preservation
