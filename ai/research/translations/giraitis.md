# Giraitis / Girstis / Girinaitis

## Comprehensive Overview

"Giraitis" is the deity or spirit of the grove (*giraitė*). While **Miškinis** or **Girinis** represents the vast, wild forest, Giraitis is a more localized and often more approachable figure associated with specific clusters of trees that were held sacred by the community.

### English (EN)
**Giraitis** is the "Guardian of the Grove." In Baltic mythology, certain groves were considered sacred and were off-limits for cutting or hunting. Giraitis was the spirit who resided within these groves, overseeing the growth of the trees and the well-being of the forest floor. He was honored with small offerings, such as bread or wool, placed at the base of the most prominent trees. Unlike the more chaotic forest spirits, Giraitis represents the ordered and sanctified aspect of nature. He is sometimes identified with **Girstis** or **Girinaitis**, representing the benevolent "Grove-Master" who ensures the spiritual peace of the woods.

### Lithuanian (LT)
**Giraitis** (arba **Girinaitis**) lietuvių mitologijoje yra giraitės dvasia arba dievas. Jį mini Janas Lasickis (*Jan Łasicki*) bei Motiejus Pretorijus (*Matthäus Prätorius*), vadindamas jį **Girsčiu** (*Girstis*). Priešingai nei bendrinis Miškinis, Giraitis yra saistomas su konkrečia, dažnai šventa laikoma lauko ar kaimo giraite. Jis saugojo miško ramybę, jo medžius ir augmeniją. Žmonės tikėjo, kad Giraičiui reikia aukoti, norint kirsti malkas ar grybauti, o šventose giraitėse bet koks triukšmas ar medžių žalojimas buvo griežtai draudžiamas. Jis personifikuoja lokalų gamtos šventumą ir žmogaus pagarbą artimiausiai miško aplinkai.

### Latvian (LV)
**Birzis** vai **Meža kungs** (Birzs māte) latviešu folklorā atbilst lietuviešu Giraitim. Tā ir garīgā būtne, kas mīt bērzu birzīs vai svētos meža iecirkņos. Giraitis ir birzs sargs, kuram dainās tiek veltītas rindas par meža tīrību un saticību ar dabu. Viņš ir mierīgāks par Meža vīru, vairāk personificējot birzs skaistumu un mieru, nevis meža mežonīgumu. Viņa loma ir sargāt tos kokus, kas atrodas tuvu cilvēka mājoklim, nodrošinot, ka starp abām pasaulēm pastāv līdzsvars.

## Primary Source Details

- **Łasicki (Łas):** Records the name *Giraitis* (Girstis) as a god of the woods and groves. [Jan Łasicki, 1615]
- **Praetorius (Prae):** Details the offerings made to the grove-god by the Samogitians and Prussians. [Matthäus Prätorius, 17th c.]
- **VLE:** Describes Giraitis as a localized forest deity, noting the overlap with Girstis.
- **Mierzyński (Mierz):** Analyzes the etymology and regional variations of the name in early documents.

## Key Metadata
- **Region**: Lithuania, Samogitia
- **Tribe**: Lithuanian, Samogitian
- **Period**: Medieval to 17th-century records
- **Category**: Nature Spirit / Grove Protector
- **Symbol**: Oak branch, Birch catkin, Sacred Grove boundary
- **Animals**: Deer, Songbirds
- **Function**: Protection of sacred groves, Ensuring tree growth, Forest peace
- **Etymology**: *gir- (forest/mountain) + *-ait- (diminutive/suffix)
