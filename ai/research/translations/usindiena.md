# Ūsiņdiena — Latvian Spring Horse-Blessing Festival

## Comprehensive Overview

**Ūsiņdiena** ("Ūsiņš Day") is the Latvian spring festival centred on the blessing of horses and the first driving of horses and cattle onto spring pasture. It falls around April 23 (St. George's Day in the Christian calendar) but is rooted in the cult of **Ūsiņš** — the Baltic deity of light, horses, bees, and spring. The festival is one of the most clearly documented pre-Christian Latvian festivals, with the earliest attestation by Dionysius Fabricius in 1610.

### English (EN)
**Ūsiņdiena** is the Latvian spring festival celebrating the revival of light, the protection of horses, and the beginning of the outdoor pasture season. The deity **Ūsiņš** — the "Lord of Light and Horses" — is formally invited to protect the horses for the entire summer season. Horses are decorated with **oak branches** and ribbons, their manes are brushed, and their stables are sprinkled with blessed water or beer. Offerings of eggs (the quintessential spring symbol) are placed in the stable: either left as offerings or boiled and fed to the horses themselves to give them strength. The first cattle and horses are driven out to the spring pasture in a ceremonial procession, with the herdswoman or farmer singing invoking songs to Ūsiņš. Bonfires are lit in the fields on the eve of Ūsiņdiena — the fires both honour Ūsiņš (as a light deity) and serve the practical purpose of warding off insects and wolves from the grazing land. Dionysius Fabricius (1610) recorded that Latvians offered silver coins to Ūsiņš and invoked him with specific prayers for the horses' health, making this one of the best-attested pre-Christian Latvian rituals.

### Lithuanian (LT)
**Ūsiņdiena** (latv., liet. *Ūsinis diena*) – latvių pavasario arklių palaiminimo šventė. **Ūsiņš** – šviesos, arklių ir bičių dievas – buvo kviečiamas apsaugoti arklius visam pavasario–vasaros ganomajam laikui. Arkliai puošiami ąžuolo šakelėmis, tverte dedami kiaušiniai kaip aukos, į pievus išvaromas gyvuliai procesijose. Fabricijaus ataskaitoje (1610 m.) dokumentuoja, kad latviai aukodavo sidabro monetas **Ūsiņui** ir melsdavosi specifiniais maldų tekstais dėl arklių sveikatos.

### Latvian (LV)
**Ūsiņdiena** ir viens no reti saglabājušies autentiskiem baltu rituāliem ar konkrētu rakstītu liecību no 1610. gada (Fabricius). Ūsiņš ir zirgu, gaismas un bišu aizgādnis, kura diena iezīmē pavasara sezonu un ganāmpulku pirmo izvešanu ganībās. Rituāli tajā dienā ietver: zirgu rotāšanu ar ozola zariem, olu upurēšanu stallī, ugunskuru kurināšanu laukā un dziesmu veltījumus Ūsiņam. Šis svētks pirms Jāņiem ir pirmais lielais lauksaimnieciskais pavasara rituāls, kurā tiek saistīta gaisma (Ūsiņš kā gaismas dievs), dzīvnieki un zeme.

## Primary Source Details

- **Fabricius (Fab):** Earliest attestation (1610) of Ūsiņdiena: "pro equis offerebant argenteum nummum Usingui" (they offered a silver coin to Ūsiņš for horses). [Dionysius Fabricius, *Livonicae historiae compendiosa series*, 1610]
- **Barons (Bar):** Numerous *dainas* invoking Ūsiņš on his festival day, including horse-blessing songs and egg-offering formulae. [Krišjānis Barons, *Latvju dainas*, 1894–1915]
- **LNE:** Article on Ūsiņdiena and its place in the Latvian ritual calendar. [*Latvijas Nacionālā enciklopēdija*]

## Key Metadata
- **Region**: Latvia-wide
- **Tribe**: Latvians
- **Period**: Pre-Christian; attested by Fabricius 1610; active folk tradition to 20th century
- **Category**: Calendar Festival / Animal-Blessing / Spring Ritual
- **Date**: Around April 23 (St. George's Day)
- **Symbol**: Oak branch, Egg offering, Silver coin, Bonfire, Decorated horse
- **Deity**: Ūsiņš (light, horses, spring, bees)
- **Ritual actions**: Decorating horses with oak branches, egg-offerings in stable, cattle/horse pasture-drive, bonfire, Ūsiņš invocation songs
