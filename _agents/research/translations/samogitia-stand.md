# Samogitian Spruce Stand / Žemaičių Eglių Giraitė / Žemaiču Egļu Audze

## Comprehensive Overview

The **Samogitian Spruce Stand** is a grove of tall spruces in Samogitia, western Lithuania, where ribbon-markers on the trees distinguish approved cutting paths from protected forest zones. The site is dedicated to **Medeina/Medinė**, the Lithuanian forest goddess and patron of hunters and woodcutters. Medeina was keeper of the forest's integrity — she could grant or deny access to timber and game, and her refusal, communicated through the hollow sound when a trunk was struck, was absolute. Samogitia maintained the most persistent pre-Christian practices in Lithuania, and the spruce stand represents the living survival of forest cult into the modern period. The ribbon system is both a practical conservation measure and a direct ritual interface with the goddess.

### English (EN)
**Medeina** was not an abstract forest personification but an active, responsive deity who could be consulted before each cutting session. The Samogitian spruce stand's ribbon system divided the grove into permitted and prohibited zones: ribbons tied to a tree marked a path as approved, while unmarked trees were under Medeina's direct protection until she signalled otherwise. Before raising any axe, woodcutters performed the knock-ritual: striking the trunk firmly with knuckles or a wooden mallet and listening carefully for the acoustic response. A hollow resonance — suggesting internal decay or a naturally hollow space — meant the tree refused to be cut; its body had already begun its transition to another phase of life and Medeina was retaining it. A solid, dense sound meant the tree's wood was intact and the goddess had not claimed it. This practice is eco-theologically sophisticated: it prevented the wasteful cutting of decayed timber while giving the conservation protocol a divine sanction that made refusal communally enforceable. Shavings from the first successfully accepted spruce were burned in a small fire, and the smoke was deliberately pushed back toward the forest interior — a direct communication to Medeina that the work had begun as promised and would proceed carefully.

### Lithuanian (LT)
**Medeina** nebuvo abstrakti miško personifikacija, bet aktyvi, reaguojanti dievybė, su kuria buvo galima pasitarti prieš kiekvieną kirtimo sesiją. Žemaičių eglių giraitės kaspinų sistema padalino giraitę į leidžiamas ir draudžiamas zonas: kaspinai, pririšti prie medžio, žymėjo taką kaip patvirtintą, o nepažymėti medžiai buvo tiesiogiai Medeinos apsaugoti, kol ji nepranešdavo kitaip. Prieš pakelidamas bet kurį kirvį, miške dirbantys darbuotojai atlikdavo beldimo ritualą: tvirtai beldė į kamieną kumščiais ar mediniu plaktuku ir atidžiai klausėsi akustinio atsakymo. Tuščias rezonansas — rodantis vidaus irimą ar natūraliai tuščia erdvę — reiškė, kad medis atsisakė būti nukirstas. Standus, tankus garsas reiškė, kad medžio mediena buvo sveika ir deivė jos nebuvo pareikalavusi.

### Latvian (LV)
**Medeina/Mežamāte** nebija abstrakta meža personifikācija, bet aktīva, reaģējoša dieviete, ar kuru varēja konsultēties pirms katras ciršanas sesijas. Žemaiču egļu audzes lentīšu sistēma sadalīja birzi atļautajās un aizliegtajās zonās: lentītes, piestiprinātas pie koka, iezīmēja celiņu kā apstiprinātu, bet nemarkēti koki bija Medeinas tiešā aizsardzībā, līdz viņa citādi signalizēja. Pirms pacelt jebkuru cirvi, mežstrādnieki veica klauvēšanas rituālu: stingri klauvēja pie stumbra ar dūriem vai koka āmuru un uzmanīgi klausījās akustisko atbildi. Dobā rezonanse nozīmēja, ka koks atteicās tikt nocirsts. Blīva skaņa nozīmēja, ka koka koks bija vesels un dieviete to nebija pieprasījusi.

## Primary Source Details

- **Hypatian Chronicle (Hyp):** First historical mention (1252) of *Medeina* as the Lithuanian forest deity consulted before military and woodcutting campaigns. [*Hypatian Ipatios Chronicle*, 1252 entry]
- **Vėlius (Vel):** Comprehensive study of Medeina's domain and the forest-deity consultation practices of Samogitian communities. [Norbertas Vėlius, *Baltų religijos ir mitologijos šaltiniai*, Vol. 1, 1996]
- **Dundulienė (Dund):** Documents the use of ribbon and sound-test systems in Lithuanian forest cult as eco-ritual conservation. [Pranė Dundulienė, *Medis senovės lietuvių tikėjimuose*, 1979]
- **Jesuit mission reports (Jes):** Note the persistent forest-deity consultation among Samogitian woodcutters in the 17th century. [Lithuanian Jesuit missions, 1569–1650]
- **Beresnevičius (Ber):** Analysis of Medeina's specific expression in Samogitian woodland cult and the sound-divination protocol. [Gintaras Beresnevičius, *Lietuvių religija ir mitologija*, 2004]
