# Apjumības — Latvian Roof-Raising Festival

## Comprehensive Overview

**Apjumības** (from *apjumt* — to roof, to cover a roof) is the Latvian ritual feast held when a new building's roof was raised and completed — one of the most significant communal building events in traditional Baltic life. It invokes **Mājas māte** (the House Mother) and **Jumis** (the double-grain spirit of abundance and completeness), who are asked to bless the newly completed structure and bring fertility and protection to its inhabitants.

### English (EN)
**Apjumības** is the Latvian roof-raising ceremony — a communal celebration held when the final beam or thatch of a new house or barn roof is laid. The entire village participates; the building is as much a communal act as an individual one. When the last roof-piece is in place, a wreath of grain-heads, birch branches, and ribbons — called the **jumis** wreath or *jumtavainags* (roof-wreath) — is placed at the roof's apex. This wreath is the physical embodiment of **Jumis** (the double-grain fertility spirit) being invited to dwell in the new building and bless it with abundance. The wreath remains on the roof to ward off lightning, disease, and evil spirits through the first winter. **Mājas māte** (the House Mother, female spirit of the dwelling's wellbeing) is formally addressed and invited into the new structure with a beer offering poured onto the threshold. The communal feast that follows is the largest beer-drinking occasion of the building year — the brewer's beer barrel is ceremonially tapped for the first time in the new building. Songs sung to the new house ask **Dievs** and **Māra** to protect its walls, and the family within it, from all harm.

### Lithuanian (LT)
**Apjumības** (latv., liet. *Stogo uždėjimas* / namo įnautinimas) — latvių naujojo pastato stogo uždėjimo šventė. Bendruomenė dalyvauja; kai paskutinis stogo elementas uždėtas, ant stogagalio pakabinama **jumta vainags** (stogo vainikas) su **Jumis** dvigubo grūdo simboliu. **Mājas māte** kviečiama į naują namą alaus libacija ant slenksčio. Puotaujama su bendruomenišku alumi; **Dievs** ir **Māra** prašomi saugoti namą ir šeimą.

### Latvian (LV)
**Apjumības** ir viens no latviešu tradicionālās būvniecības galvenajiem rituāliem. Kad jumts ir pabeigts, uz tā gaisa jumtavainags — graudu gvairojums ar ozola zariem, ko dāvā **Jumim** (labības garam) kā uzaicinājuma žests. Mājas māte tiek uzaicināta ar alus libāciju pie sliekšņa, nocenēta ar dziesmām un lūgšanām. Kopienas mielasts ar īpaši brūvētu alu nozīmē, ka arī kaimiņi ir piedalījušies jauna dzīvesspēka veidošanā. Apjumības parāda, ka ēkas celtniecība bija sakrāls process, kurā cilvēks, zeme un dievu aizbildniecība saskārās.

## Primary Source Details

- **Barons (Bar):** *Dainas* associated with roof-raising and house-blessing ceremonies, including Mājas māte invocations. [Krišjānis Barons, *Latvju dainas*, 1894–1915]
- **Šmits (Šmits):** Documents the *jumtavainags* tradition and the Apjumības feast structure. [Pēteris Šmits, *Latviešu mitoloģija*, 1926]

## Key Metadata
- **Region**: Latvia-wide
- **Tribe**: Latvians
- **Period**: Pre-Christian; documented in folk-custom records from 17th century onward
- **Category**: Lifecycle / Building Ritual / Communal Festival
- **Date**: Occasion-based (when a new building's roof is completed)
- **Symbol**: Roof-wreath (*jumtavainags*), Double-grain (*Jumis*), Beer barrel, Threshold libation
- **Deities invoked**: Mājas māte (house mother), Jumis (fertility/abundance), Dievs (protection), Māra (dwelling-guardian)
- **Ritual actions**: Roof-wreath placement, threshold beer-pouring for Mājas māte, communal feast, house-blessing songs
- **References**:
    - "Barons, Krišjānis: Latvju dainas (1894–1915)"
    - "Šmits, Pēteris: Latviešu mitoloģija (1918)"
