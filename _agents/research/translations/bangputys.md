# Bangpūtys

## Comprehensive Overview

Bangpūtys is the Lithuanian (and shared Baltic) personification of the sea, waves, and winds. His name is directly derived from "banga" (wave) and "pūsti" (to blow).

### English (EN)
**Bangpūtys** is the "Wave-Blower." He is the master of the sea and the guardian of its depths. In Baltic mythology, the sea was an austere and dangerous domain, and Bangpūtys was worshipped to appease the waves and ensure the safety of those on the water. In later scholarly reconstructions (primarily Matthäus Prätorius, 17th c., and subsequent Lithuanian mythographers), he is depicted as a two-faced deity (an iconographic tradition, by analogy with the Roman Janus), winged, and sometimes with a fish in his left hand, a rudder in his right, and a rooster on his head. He is described in reconstructed tradition as the father of the four wind personifications: **Rytys** (East), **Pietys** (South), **Šiaurys** (North), and **Vakaris** (West) — an attribution found in later scholarly sources rather than medieval primary texts. Offerings were made to him before setting sail or when the sea was rough to grant calm waters.

### Lithuanian (LT)
**Bangpūtys** lietuvių mitologijoje yra jūrų, marių ir bangų dievas. Jo vardas kilo iš „bangos pūtimas“. Jis vaizduojamas kaip ramybės ir audrų valdovas, kuriam paklūsta visi vandenys. Vėlesnėje rekonstruotoje tradicijoje (pirmiausia Matthäus Prätorius, XVII a.) jis vaizduojamas su dviem veidais, sparnais, žuvimi kairėje rankoje ir gaidžiu ant galvos. Bangpūtys šioje rekonstruotoje tradicijoje laikomas keturių vėjų (Ryčio, Piečio, Šiaurio ir Vakario) tėvu. Jis simbolizuoja darnos ir baimės ryšį su galinguoju vandens stichija.

### Latvian (LV)
**Bangpūtys** latviešu mitoloģijā nav ticis plaši izdalīts, tā vietā jūras un viļņu sargāšanas funkcijas veica dažādas **Jūras mātes** vai **Vēja māte** izpausmes. Tomēr lietuviešu tradīcijās un senu sarakstu kontekstā Bangpūtys reprezentē jūras un vētru aizbildni, kura ziņā ir „uzpūst” viļņus vai tos nomierināt, simbolizējot rūpību un dabas sniegto velšu aizsardzību. Viņš ir vēju tēvs un ūdens stihijas valdnieks.

## Primary Source Details

- **Łasicki (Łas):** Records a sea deity among Lithuanian spirits (*pro undas*); the specific identification with *Bangputti* is found in some scholarly readings of this text. [Jan Łasicki, 1615] *(Attribution requires verification against the 1615 published text.)*
- **Vėlius (Vel):** Analyzed the structure of the water-tier in Baltic mythology, noting the functional role of the "Wave-Blower" spirits. [Norbertas Vėlius, 1996]
- **VLE:** Confirms the identification of Bangpūtys as a sea deity and discusses his mythological significance and iconographic features. [Visuotinė lietuvių enciklopedija]
- **Stryjkowski (Str):** Mentions him among the Baltic maritime spirits. [Maciej Stryjkowski, 1582]
