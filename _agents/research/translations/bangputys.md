# Bangpūtys

## Comprehensive Overview

Bangpūtys is the Lithuanian (and shared Baltic) personification of the sea, waves, and winds. His name is directly derived from "banga" (wave) and "pūsti" (to blow).

### English (EN)
**Bangpūtys** is the "Wave-Blower." He is the master of the sea and the guardian of its depths. In Baltic mythology, the sea was an austere and dangerous domain, and Bangpūtys was worshipped to appease the waves and ensure the safety of those on the water. He was often depicted as a two-faced or multi-faced deity (similar to Janus), reflecting his ability to see across the vast expanse of the ocean. He is the guardian of the sea's bounty and its destructive power. Offerings were made to him before setting sail or when the sea was rough.

### Lithuanian (LT)
**Bangpūtys** lietuvių mitologijoje yra jūrų, marių ir bangų dievas. Jo vardas kilo iš „bangos pūtimas“. Jis vaizduojamas kaip ramybės ir audrų valdovas, kuriam paklūsta visi vandenys. Janas Lasickis nurodė, kad šiam dievui būdavo meldžiamasi norint nulemti gerą žvejybą ir saugią kelionę per marias. Bangpūtys simbolizuoja darnos ir baimės ryšį su galinguoju vandens stichija, kuri yra ne tik maitintoja, bet ir neprognozuojama jėga.

### Latvian (LV)
**Bangpūtys** latviešu mitoloģijā nav ticis plaši izdalīts, tā vietā jūras un viļņu sargāšanas funkcijas veica dažādas **Jūras mātes** vai **Vēja mātest** izpausmes. Tomēr lietuviešu tradīcijas un lokālo sarakstu kontekstā Bangpūtys reprezentē jūras un vētru aizbildni, kura ziņā ir „uzpūst” viļņus vai tos nomierināt, simbolizējot rūpību un dabas sniegto velšu aizsardzību.

## Primary Source Details

- **Łasicki (Łas):** Records *Bangpūtį* as a deity associated with the sea (*pro undas*), describing the supplications made for a calm voyage.
- **Vėlius (Vel):** Analyzed the structure of the water-tier in Baltic mythology, noting the functional role of the "Wave-Blower" spirits.
- **VLE:** Confirms the identification of Bangpūtys as a sea deity and discusses his mythological significance.

## Key Metadata
- **Category**: Nature / Water Deity
- **Symbol**: Wave, Trident, Seaweed, Ship, Two-faced head
- **Function**: Protection of those on the sea and control of storms
- **Etymology**: *banga (wave) + *pūsti (to blow)
