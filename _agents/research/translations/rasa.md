# Rasa

## Comprehensive Overview

Rasa is the Lithuanian personification of the morning dew and the freshness of the dawn. In Baltic folklore, she is often considered a daughter or handmaiden of the Sun goddess, **Saulė**, and is the goddess of summer vegetation and flowers.

### English (EN)
**Rasa** is the "Goddess of Dew." She represents the life-giving moisture that appears on plants at dawn, believed to have healing and purifying properties. In mythological cycles, Rasa is the one who washes the face of the Sun each morning, ensuring her radiance. She is the protector of the meadows and the delicate blossoms of the early summer. Her name is synonymous with the midsummer festival rituals (**Rasos šventė**), where the gathering of dew and the washing of faces was a central act of spiritual renewal and fertility magic.

### Lithuanian (LT)
**Rasa** lietuvių mitologijoje yra ryto rasos, augmenijos gaivos ir vasaros žalumos deivė. Pasak sakmių, ji yra **Saulės** dukra, kuri kasryt prausia motinos veidą, suteikdama jai spindesio. Rasa simbolizuoja gyvybingumą, tyrumą ir gydomąją vandens galią. Jos vardu pavadinta tradicinė vasaros saulėgrįžos šventė (**Rasos**), kurios metu tikima, kad surinkta rasa turi magiškų galių – gydo akis, skaistina veidą ir gausina derlių.

### Latvian (LV)
**Rasa** latviešu mitoloģijā ir rīta rasas personifikācija, **Saules meita**, kura dainās bieži parādās saistībā ar rīta cēlienu un dabas atmošanos. Rasa simbolizē tīrību un dabas dzīvības spēku, kas nepieciešams katram asnam un ziedam. Latviešu folklorā rasa tiek uzskatīta par svētītu ūdeni, ko debesu būtnes izmanto, lai atvēsinātu zemi pēc karstas vasaras dienas. Viņa ir miera un rīta svaiguma aizbildne.

## Primary Source Details

- **Folk Tradition:** The primary source for Rasa is the extensive oral record of the *Rasos* (Midsummer) festival and its associated dainas and rituals.
- **Vėlius (Vel):** Analyzed the "dew" motif in Baltic mythology, highlighting its role as a bridge between the celestial and terrestrial worlds.
- **Barons (Bar):** Cataloged numerous Latvian dainas mentioning the "blessing of the dew" (rasas svētība).
- **VLE:** Confirms Rasa's status as a personification of the morning dew and a goddess of vegetation.
