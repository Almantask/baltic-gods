# Grabnyčios — Lithuanian Candlemas

## Comprehensive Overview

**Grabnyčios** is the Lithuanian Candlemas festival, observed on February 2. The name derives from *grabnycė* (a special wax candle blessed on this day). In Catholic tradition this is the Feast of the Presentation of Jesus at the Temple; in older Baltic practice, February 2 was a threshold date marking the first loosening of winter's grip and the reaffirmation of the sacred hearth fire's power against a still-dark world.

### English (EN)
**Grabnyčios** (Candlemas, February 2) is the Lithuanian mid-winter candle festival, marking the halfway point between the winter solstice and the spring equinox. Its central ritual is the blessing of large wax candles (*grabnyčios žvakės*) in church or at the home hearth. These candles have multiple protective functions: lit during thunderstorms, they protect the house from lightning (invoking **Perkūnas**); held by the dying, they ease the passage of the soul and ward off **Giltinė** (the Death Goddess) from claiming the living prematurely. A grab-candle placed in the window signals to **Gabija** that the family is observant and deserving of protection. The Grabnyčios candle kept throughout the year was a powerful apotropaic object — a crystallisation of sacred fire — used in healing rituals, first-plow blessings, and at deathbeds. In folk belief, a warm February 2 signalled a late and harsh spring; a cold day meant an early warm season — the date functioned as a solar prognostic marker.

### Lithuanian (LT)
**Grabnyčios** – vasario 2 d. švenčiama žvakių pašventinimo šventė, kurioje senasis ikikrikščioniškasis žiemos lūžtamo laiko džiaugsmas susipina su katalikybe. Tą dieną pašventinamos grabnyčios žvakės, naudojamos metų bėgyje: griaustinio metu – nuo žaibo, prie mirštančiojo – nuo **Giltinės**, pirmojo arimo metu – **Žemynai** pagerbti. Žvakė yra šventosios ugnies įkūnijimas: jos šviesoje matoma **Gabija**. Liaudies pranašystės byloja, kad vasario 2-osios oras nusprendžia, kokia bus pavasario pradžia — tai gamtos prognostikos tradicija, tiesiogiai susijusi su saule ir žemės atbudimu.

### Latvian (LV)
**Grabnyčios** latviešu paralēle ir saistīta ar februāra ugunskuru un svecīšu tradīcijām, tomēr tiešs ekvivalents nav nostiprināts. Latviešu tradīcijā *Meteņi* (Šrovetide) ir tuvākais svētks šajā laikā, kas arī iezīmē pāreju no dziļās ziemas uz pirmspavasari. Abās tradīcijās svece (uguns simbols) ir galvenais instruments — tā vienlaicīgi saista cilvēku ar dievu uguns spēku un ar senču pasauli.

## Key Metadata
- **Region**: Lithuania-wide
- **Tribe**: Lithuanians, Samogitians
- **Period**: Pre-Christian mid-winter threshold feast; syncretic with Catholic Candlemas from 15th century
- **Category**: Calendar Festival / Fire / Protective Ritual
- **Date**: February 2
- **Symbol**: Wax candle (*grabnyčia*), Hearth fire, Thunder-protective object
- **Deities invoked**: Gabija (hearth fire), Perkūnas (lightning protection), Giltinė (death-warding)
- **Ritual actions**: Candle blessing, storm-protection rites, deathbed candle-holding, spring-prognostication
