# Nature Mātes / Mother Spirits of Water, Wind, and Weather

## Comprehensive Overview

In Latvian mythology, the "Mātes" (Mothers) are personifications of the various forces and phenomena of the natural world. This category includes the sovereign spirits of the sea, the air, and the atmospheric conditions that governed the survival of the ancient Baltic people.

### Jūras māte (Mother of the Sea)
**Jūras māte** is the sovereign of the Baltic Sea. In the Latvian *dainas*, she is depicted as a powerful mistress who commands the depth and the waves (her "servants"). She lives in a palace of amber on the sea floor and is the guardian of all sea-wealth. Fishermen made offerings to her for safety and a good catch. She is a dualistic figure: a provider of food but also a fierce taker of lives if she is not respected or if the winds provoke her.
- **Symbol**: Sea-foam, Amber, Fishing net, Wave-crest
- **Region**: Coastal Latvia (Kurzeme, Vidzeme)

### Vēja māte (Mother of the Wind)
**Vēja māte** regulates the air and the four cardinal winds. She is often seen as a brisk, elderly woman who shakes the treetops and powers the sailboats. She is invoked by sailors to "blow" them home and by farmers to winnow the grain. In some traditions, she is the mother of the four wind-sons. She represents the vital breath of nature but can turn into a destructive storm if angered. 
- **Symbol**: Wind-vane, Tree-tops in motion, Cloud-streaks
- **Category**: Atmospheric Spirit

### Ūdens māte & Upes māte (Mothers of Water and Rivers)
**Ūdens māte** is the general spirit of freshwater (lakes, springs, wells), while **Upes māte** is specifically associated with the flowing water of rivers. They are nurturers of the land's life-blood. Latvian women brought offerings of beads, coins, or woven ribbons to the riverbanks to ensure the purity of the water. Upes māte is also associated with life transitions, as flowing water represents the passage of time and the journey of the soul.
- **Symbol**: Spring-well, Riverbend, Silver water-droplets

### Weather Sisters (Lietus, Miglas, Salna, Bangu)
- **Lietus māte** (Mother of Rain): The giver of moisture; her "tears" or "poured water" nourish the crops.
- **Miglas māte** (Mother of Mist): The mistress of the fog. She creates the liminal, "hidden" world where boundaries between the living and the dead are blurred. Travelers avoid her path to keep from being lost.
- **Salna māte** (Mother of Frost): The "biter" of plants. She appears in early spring and late autumn, representing the transient cold that tests the resilience of the crops.
- **Bangu māte** (Mother of Waves): Often considered an aspect or daughter of Jūras māte, specifically presiding over the churning or "banging" motion of the waves against the shore.

## Primary Source Details

- **Kr. Barons (Bar):** The primary collector of dainas where these spirits are most vividly described. [Latvju dainas, 1894–1915]
- **P. Šmits (Šmit):** Systematized the Latvian mythological figures in his research. [Latviešu mitoloģija, 1918]
- **LNE:** Provides modern ethnographic analysis of the "Māte" complex.
- **Biezais (Biez):** Examines the structural roles of the Mothers in the celestial family. [Haralds Biezais, 1972]

## Key Metadata
- **Region**: Latvia (Pan-Latvian, with coastal focus for Jūras māte)
- **Period**: Archaic / Oral Tradition
- **Category**: Nature Spirits / Mother Spirits (Mātes)
- **Function**: Regulating natural elements, Protecting specific ecosystems, Receiving communal offerings
