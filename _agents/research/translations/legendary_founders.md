# Lithuanian Legendary Figures & Mythic Founders / The Sovereigns of Story

## Comprehensive Overview

The transition from the primordial panteon to the historical state of Lithuania is marked by legendary figures who established the core traditions of the nation. These figures inhabit the liminal space between human memory and divine mystery.

### Eglė (The Queen of Serpents)
**Eglė** is the heroine of Lithuania's most profound and archaic myth-folktale, *Eglė žalčių karalienė*. She is a maiden who marries the serpent king **Žilvinas** and lives in his underwater palace. The myth is a theogonic and nature-origin story; after the betrayal and murder of her husband by her brothers, Eglė and her children undergo a metamorphosis into trees. Eglė becomes a spruce (*eglė*), while her children become the oak, ash, birch, and aspen. The myth underscores the sacred, ancestral bond between humans and the chthonic serpent world.
- **Symbol**: Spruce tree, Snake-crown, Underwater palace, The names of her children (Ąžuolas, Uosis, Beržas, Drebulė).
- **Theme**: Metamorphosis, Sacrality of the oath, The origin of Baltic forests.

### Žilvinas (The Serpent King)
**Žilvinas** is the sovereign of the waters and the husband of Eglė. He embodies the dual nature of the sacred snake (**Žaltys**): a frightening beast in the wild, but a noble, handsome ruler in the divine realm. He is the provider of material abundance and the guardian of the depths. His tragic death is the catalyst for the myth's final transformation, representing the permanent separation of the human and the supernatural worlds.
- **Symbol**: Silver/Grey serpent, Sea-palace, Willow sapling (*žilvis*)
- **Role**: King of the Serpents, Bestower of prosperity.

### Sovijus (The Founder of Cremation)
**Sovijus** is a legendary psychopomp and cultural hero mentioned in the 13th-century *Malalas Chronicle*. He is credited with establishing the funeral customs of the Baltic people. The myth describes his journey to the underworld after death and his search for a resting place. Finding that burial in the earth or water was "painful" (cold or claustrophobic), he chose the fire as the only way to reach a comfortable afterlife. By establishing cremation, Sovijus opened the "gate" to the divine realm for all subsequent generations.
- **Symbol**: Funeral pyre, Gate of the dead, Descent into the underworld.
- **Role**: Founder of Death Customs, First Mortal to return.

### Šventaragis (The King of the Sacred Valley)
**Šventaragis** is a legendary Grand Duke of Lithuania who sacralized the site of modern-day Vilnius. According to the *Bychowiec Chronicle*, he requested that his body be cremated at the confluence of the Neris and Vilnia rivers. This valley, thereafter known as **Šventaragis' Valley**, became the official mausoleum and cremation site for the pagan rulers of Lithuania. He represents the fusion of political sovereignty with the sacred fire-cult.
- **Symbol**: The Valley of Šventaragis, Confluence of rivers, Royal pyre.
- **Role**: Sacral founder of Vilnius, Guardian of the royal flame.

## Primary Source Details

- **Lithuanian Chronicles (Chron):** The primary record of the legend of Šventaragis and the founding of the Vilnius cult. [Bychowiec Chronicle, 16th c.]
- **Malalas Chronicle (Malal):** Contains the 13th-century Slavic interpolation of the Sovijus myth.
- **Basanavičius (Bas):** Collected numerous variations of the Eglė myth from oral tradition.
- **Beresnevičius (Ber):** Analyzed the "death-custom" foundations of the Sovijus and Šventaragis legends. [Lietuvių religija ir mitologija, 2004]

## Key Metadata
- **Region**: Lithuania (Neris valley / Curonian coast)
- **Period**: Archaic (Eglė/Sovijus) to Legendary-Historical (Šventaragis)
- **Category**: Legendary Figures / Mythic Founders / Psychopomps
- **Function**: Origin of trees (Eglė), Origin of cremation (Sovijus), Sacralizing the capital (Šventaragis)
