# Meteņi — Latvian Pre-Spring Carnival

## Comprehensive Overview

**Meteņi** (also *Aizgavēnis* in some regions) is the Latvian pre-spring carnival festival, analogous to the Lithuanian **Užgavėnės** and to pan-European Shrovetide traditions. Celebrated in February or early March, it marks the turning-point of winter and channels the community's collective will to drive out cold and barrenness and invite spring's fertility. Like its Lithuanian counterpart, it features sleigh-racing, mask-wearing, feasting, and the symbolic defeat of winter.

### English (EN)
**Meteņi** is the Latvian Shrovetide carnival, observed when winter is at its most exhausting and the first signs of spring's approach are longed for. The festival is characterised by energetic **horse-drawn sleigh-riding** (*braukšana ar zirgiem*) — the long sleigh runs were believed to "pull" and lengthen the flax fibres for the coming summer textile season (sympathetic magic). Women prepared and ate large quantities of fatty foods — peas, beans, pig's trotters, blood sausage — to ensure that livestock and people would have fat and strength for the spring work ahead. Masked figures (*ķekatas* or *buļļi*) visit farms, representing chthonic forces, last year's deceased, and winter spirits; their visit is welcomed with food and drink that must not be refused. The last days of Meteņi coincide with the death of winter: some regions burned a straw effigy (*ziemas tēls*, winter's image) at the end of the festival, identical in structure to Lithuanian Morė-burning. Songs address the sowing gods, asking them to prepare the ground and granting speed to the departing winter.

### Lithuanian (LT)
**Meteņi** (latv., liet. analogas *Užgavėnės*) — latvių karnavalinė šventė vasarį–kovo pradžioje, skirta žiemą pabaidyti. Ilgi rogių važiavimai simboliškai „tempia" linų pluoštus; valgomi riebūs maistai, kad žmonės ir gyvuliai stiprūs; kaukių persirengėliai apvaikštinėja ūkius. Regione kartais deginamas žiemos izovas kaip ir lietuvių **Morė**. Artimiausias paralėlis tarp lietuvių ir latvių karnavalo tradicijų, liudijantis protobaltų bendrą žiemos galutinio išvarymo ritualo šaknį.

### Latvian (LV)
**Meteņi** ir Latvijas ziemas karnevāls un viens no lielākajiem tautas svētkiem pirms pavasara. Tas izsaka kolektīvo vēlmi pēc pavasara caur ritualiem: zirgu braukšana ar kamanām (*slīdēšana*), treknā ēšana (cūkgaļa, zirņi, asinsdesа), masku gājieni un dziesmas par ziemas padzīšanu. Meteņos tiek godāts arī linu un audumu cikls — kam tīra slīdpiste ar kamanām, tas pastiepj arī linus. Svētki bieži beidzas ar limonadēšanu uz ledus vai dziesmu par pavasara aktoru ierašanos. Latviešu tradīcijā Meteņi un Ziemassvētki ir divi galvenie ziemas karnevāla punkti.

## Primary Source Details

- **Barons (Bar):** Collected numerous *dainas* associated with Meteņi, including sleigh-racing songs and winter-farewell verses. [Krišjānis Barons, *Latvju dainas*, 1894–1915]
- **Šmits (Šmits):** Documents the masked-mummer tradition (*ķekatas*), sleigh-magic for linen, and regional Meteņi customs. [Pēteris Šmits, *Latviešu mitoloģija*, 1926]

## Key Metadata
- **Region**: Latvia-wide
- **Tribe**: Latvians
- **Period**: Pre-Christian origin; documented from 18th-century folk sources
- **Category**: Calendar Festival / Seasonal Transition / Carnival Ritual
- **Date**: Shrovetide (February–early March, moveable)
- **Symbol**: Sleigh (*kamanas*), Linen thread, Pig symbol, Winter effigy (*ziemas tēls*), Mask
- **Ritual actions**: Horse-sleigh racing (flax-magic), feasting on fatty foods, ķekatas mummer visits, winter-effigy burning, spring invocation songs
