# Pirmoji arba — Lithuanian First Plow Day Ritual

## Comprehensive Overview

**Pirmoji arba** ("the first plowing") is the Lithuanian spring ritual marking the first turning of the soil after winter — the moment the earth is ceremonially opened for the agricultural year. It is not a calendar festival with a fixed date but an occasion determined by the farmer's judgment of soil readiness, typically in April, and is one of the most intimate and theologically significant of Baltic agricultural rites.

### English (EN)
**Pirmoji arba** is the Lithuanian first-plowing ceremony, performed when the farmer judges the ground has thawed enough to accept the plow. Before the first furrow is cut, **Žemyna** (the Earth Goddess) must be formally addressed: the farmer kneels, touches the soil, and pours a small libation (beer, mead, or milk) directly onto the earth while reciting an invocation — "Žemyna, mother, open your body to receive the seed." The first furrow is a sacred act of cosmic partnership: the plow's blade is the male principle penetrating Žemyna's body; the seed is the divine promise. **Žemėpatis** (the Farmstead Lord) and **Lauksargis** (the Field Guardian) are also invoked to protect the boundary between plowed and unplowed ground during this vulnerable opening moment. A piece of bread or cheese was placed at the start of the first furrow as an offering that the plow would turn into the earth, burying it as a gift. Laima (Fate) is present as an unseen observer: the straightness and smoothness of the first furrow was read as an omen for the coming harvest.

### Lithuanian (LT)
**Pirmoji arba** – pavasario apeiga, kurios metu ūkininkas pirmą kartą po žiemos aria žemę. Prieš pirmą vagą jis parbyla ant kelių, liečia žemę ir pila libaciją (alų, midų arba pieną), tapdamas į **Žemyną**: „Žemynėle, motinėle, atsidaryk priimti sėklą..." Pirmas sąvartos yra sakralus partnerystės aktas su žeme. **Žemėpatis** ir **Lauksargis** kviečiami apsaugoti dieną. Po arimo pirmame vagoje paliekamas duonos ar sūrio gabalas kaip auka. Pirmosios vagos tiesumas – Laimos likimo ženklas metams.

### Latvian (LV)
**Pirmā aršana** (latviešu paralēle) satur identiskas ceremonijas — latviešu zemnieks arī pieskaras zemei, lūdzas **Zemes mātei** un pielej libāciju pirms pirmās vagas. Latviešu folklorā ir saglabājušās daudzas dainaspar pirmās aršanas svētumu un tās saistību ar saules atgriešanos un zemes pamošanos. Abās tradīcijās arājs ir ne tikai zemkopis, bet arī priestera figūra, kas ar kulta darbību atver gadu un ieaicina ražu.

## Primary Source Details

- **Folk tradition:** Ethnographic records of first-plow prayers and libations across Lithuanian regions, preserved by regional collectors.
- **Vėlius (Vel2):** Documents the first-plow ritual as a key moment in the agricultural sacred-calendar, including the specific verbal formula of the Žemyna address. [Norbertas Vėlius, *Senovės baltų pasaulėžiūra*, 1983]
- **Beresnevičius (Ber):** Analyses the first-plow as a hierogamic (sacred marriage) act between the farmer and Žemyna. [Gintaras Beresnevičius, 2004]

## Key Metadata
- **Region**: Lithuania-wide (rural farming communities)
- **Tribe**: Lithuanians
- **Period**: Proto-Baltic; documented in folk custom from 17th-century visitors' reports
- **Category**: Agricultural Ritual / Sacred Opening Ceremony
- **Date**: Spring (April, variable — whenever soil is ready)
- **Symbol**: Plow blade, First furrow, Libation vessel, Buried bread-offering
- **Deities invoked**: Žemyna (earth), Žemėpatis (farmstead lord), Lauksargis (field guardian), Laima (fate omen)
- **Ritual actions**: Kneeling at field edge, Žemyna invocation, libation pouring, first furrow as sacred act, buried offering
- **References**:
    - "Vėlius, Norbertas: Senovės baltų pasaulėžiūra (1983)"
    - "Beresnevičius, Gintaras: Lietuvių religija ir mitologija (2004)"
