# Dimstipatis

## Comprehensive Overview

Dimstipatis (Lithuanian) is a prominent Baltic deity of the homestead, farmstead, and household boundaries. His name is derived from "dimstis" (homestead) and "patis" (lord/master).

### English (EN)
**Dimstipatis** is the "Lord of the Homestead." He is the masculine guardian of the household boundaries and the divine master of the farmstead. Unlike **Žemėpatis**, who is the master of the cultivated land, Dimstipatis focuses on the physical buildings and the security of the family's home. He was believed to reside near the hearth or near the entrance, ensuring that no evil spirits or misfortunes entered the living space. He was the protector against house fires and the guardian of the family's continuity and safety.

### Lithuanian (LT)
**Dimstipatis** lietuvių mitologijoje yra sodybos ir namų šventovės dievas, kurio vardas kilo iš „dimsties“ (sodyba) ir „patis“ (šeimininkas). Janas Lasickis nurodė, kad šiam dievui būdavo meldžiamasi norint nulemti namų ramybę ir apsaugą nuo gaisrų (*domus custos*). Dimstipatis simbolizuoja kantrybę, tvarką ir sodybos, kaip sakralios erdvės, vientisumą. Jis buvo ypač svarbus naujakuriams ir šeimos sėkmei.

### Latvian (LV)
**Dimstipatis** latviešu mitoloģijā nav ticis atsevišķi izdalīts, tā vietā viņa funkcijas veica dažādas **Mājas kunga** izpausmes. Tomēr lietuviešu tradīcijai ir lokālo sarakstu kontekstā Dimstipatis reprezentē sētas un namdurvju aizbildni, kura ziņā ir nosargāt sētas mieru un nodrošināt sētas robežu aizsardzību, simbolizējot rūpību un dabas sniegto velšu aizsardzību.

## Primary Source Details

- **Łasicki (Łas):** Records *Dimstipatis* as a major household deity (*domus custos*) invoked for the protection of property and the family.
- **Vėlius (Vel):** Analyzed Dimstipatis's role as a mediator between the human world and the divine, noting the functional split between the morning and evening servants of the home.
- **VLE:** Confirms the identification of Dimstipatis as a deity of the homestead.

## Key Metadata
- **Category**: Household / Domestic Deity
- **Symbol**: Hearth, Entrance, Farmstead boundary, House-keys
- **Function**: Protection of the family's home and property
- **Etymology**: *dimstis (homestead) + *patis (lord/master)
