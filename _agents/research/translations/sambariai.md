# Sambariai — Lithuanian Autumn Communal Harvest Feast

## Comprehensive Overview

**Sambariai** (also *sambūriai* — "gatherings") are the Lithuanian communal autumn harvest feasts, held after the main grain harvest was complete, typically in September or October. These collective celebrations brought the entire village together to give thanks to the earth, drink communal beer, and acknowledge the transition into the darker half of the year. They are documented by Norbertas Vėlius as one of the oldest forms of community ritual in Lithuanian folk life.

### English (EN)
**Sambariai** are the Lithuanian communal harvest feasts — collective village gatherings marking the end of the agricultural year. Once the grain was safely stored, the community assembled on a communal threshing floor or in the farmstead's largest barn. A large cauldron of beer (*alus*) was brewed specifically for the occasion, often dedicated to the brewing deity **Ragutis** (or the female **Ragutienė**). A ritual toast was made to **Žemyna** (Earth), who had provided the harvest; bread and the first milled flour were presented to the spirit of the barn (**Gabjauja**). Food was shared communally — roasted meats, bread, cheese — in a spirit of collective thanksgiving. Songs and dances celebrated the completed year's work. The communal character of Sambariai was essential: they reinforced social bonds, settled debts between neighbours, and spiritually closed the agricultural year before the period of darkness and ancestor-commemoration began. The last sheaf of the harvest (the **Jievaras**) was sometimes carried to the Sambariai feast as the centrepiece of the thanksgiving.

### Lithuanian (LT)
**Sambariai** – tai rudeniniai bendruomeniniai derliaus šventės susirinkimai. Po javapjūtės kaimą rinko vienoje apylinkėje, kur buvo brūžama bendra alaus statinė ir dainuojama **Žemynai** ir **Ragučiui**. Aruodų globėja **Gabjauja** buvo pagerbta pirmuoju miltų plonu. Paskutinis javų pėdas (**Jievaras**) dažnai nešiojamas į sambūrį kaip padėkos centras. Norbertas Vėlius (*Vel2*) ir Gintaras Beresnevičius (*Ber*) nurodo, kad šie sambūriai buvo esminis bendruomeninio ryšio atnaujinimo aktas prieš žiemos sezono pradžią ir mirusiųjų ypatingą svarbos laiką.

### Latvian (LV)
**Miķeļi** (latviešu paralēle rudens ražas novākšanai) un **Apjumības** (jumta celšanas svētki, kopīgs darbs) atbilst lietuviešu Sambariai garam — abi ir kopīgo zemkopju darbu un pateicības rīti. Latviešu ražas noslēguma ceremonijas arī ietver komunālu alu, pēdējo vārpu lauka upurēšanu un dziesmas par zemes bagātību. Kopīgais elements — komunālā alus muca, kas tiek svētīta vārdu un dziesmu veltījumos Zemei un lauku dievietēm.

## Primary Source Details

- **Vėlius (Vel2):** Documents the communal harvest-feast structure (Sambariai) as a core feature of the Lithuanian agricultural ritual calendar. [Norbertas Vėlius, *Senovės baltų pasaulėžiūra*, 1983]
- **Beresnevičius (Ber):** Analyses the social function of Sambariai as communal bond-renewal before ancestor season. [Gintaras Beresnevičius, 2004]
- **Folk tradition:** Song, prayer, and custom records from ethnographic collections relating to communal harvest beer and grain thanksgiving.

## Key Metadata
- **Region**: Lithuania-wide
- **Tribe**: Lithuanians
- **Period**: Proto-Baltic; attested in folk custom from 17th century ethnographic sources
- **Category**: Calendar Festival / Agricultural / Communal Ritual
- **Date**: Late September–October (after grain harvest)
- **Symbol**: Beer barrel (*alus*), Last sheaf (*Jievaras*), Bread loaf, Threshing floor
- **Deities invoked**: Žemyna (earth), Ragutis/Ragutienė (brewing), Gabjauja (barns), Jievaras (last-sheaf spirit)
- **Ritual actions**: Communal beer-brewing, Žemyna toast, Jievaras procession, harvest songs
- **References**:
    - "Vėlius, Norbertas: Senovės baltų pasaulėžiūra (1983)"
    - "Beresnevičius, Gintaras: Lietuvių religija ir mitologija (2004)"
