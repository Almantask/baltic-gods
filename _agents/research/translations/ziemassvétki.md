# Ziemassvētki — Latvian Winter Solstice Festival

## Comprehensive Overview

**Ziemassvētki** ("Winter Festival" or "Winter Sacred Days") is the Latvian winter solstice and Christmas festival, observed December 21–26. While now merged with Christian Christmas, its pre-Christian foundations are deeply embedded in the Latvian ritual calendar. The festival centres on the rebirth of the sun (*Saulīte*), the burning of the **Bluķis** (Yule log), masked mummers known as **Ķekatas** or **Budēļi**, and the ancestral veneration that links the longest night to the memory of the dead.

### English (EN)
**Ziemassvētki** is the Latvian midwinter festival — the cosmic pivot between the dying and reborn sun. The most dramatic ritual is the **Bluķis** (log or block): a heavy log is decorated, paraded around the farmstead, and then ceremonially dragged around the outbuildings on the solstice eve to sweep evil spirits from all corners; it is then burned in the courtyard or hearth fire, its sustained burning through the night magically ensuring the sun's return. **Ķekatas** (masked mummers) — wearing animal masks, grotesque faces, and inverted fur coats — travel from farm to farm, singing blessings and demanding food and hospitality. They embody ancestral spirits and chthonic forces doing a last winter circuit. Families gather for a communal feast; extra food is set out for the dead. Songs specifically for *Ziemassvētku* night invoke **Dievs** (the sky father) and **Saule** (the sun) to return and resume their patronage of the living world. The festival is deeply linked to the twelve-day period *between* solstice and the new year, during which the world hovers in liminal darkness before the solar year restarts.

### Lithuanian (LT)
**Ziemassvētki** (latv., liet. analogas *Kaladų vakaras* / *Kūčios*) — latvių žiemos saulėgrįžos šventė, gruodžio 21–26 d. Pagrindinis ritualas – **Bluķis** (birzgė): puoštas rąstas vežiojamas aplink sodybą, nupučiant piktas dvasias, o po to deginamas. **Ķekatas** – kaukių persirengėliai – apvaikštinėja ūkius ir dainuoja palaiminimus. Šventė sujungia mirusiųjų pagerbimą, saulės atgimą ir bendruomeninį puotą – trys jungties veiksniai, atpažįstami visuose baltų žiemos švenčių analuose.

### Latvian (LV)
**Ziemassvētki** ir nozīmīgākie Latvijas ziemas svētki, kas apvieno saules atgriešanās rituālu, senču godāšanu un kopienas saietu. Bluķa rituāls — zirgu kamanu brauciens ar rotātu baļķi apkārt sētas ēkām — ir unikāls latvisks elements, kas nav atrodams tik detalizēti citās baltiskas tautās. Ķekatas ir mirušo un meža koku garu aizstāvji, kas veic pēdējo apkārtgaitu pirms ziemas dziļākā punkta. Dievsesv un Saules dziesmās šajā naktī tiek lūgts, lai saule atgrieztos, lai kauli nesaltu un lai lauks nākamgad nestu ražu.

## Primary Source Details

- **Barons (Bar):** Large corpus of *Ziemassvētku* dainas invoking Dievs and Saule at the winter solstice. [Krišjānis Barons, *Latvju dainas*, 1894–1915]
- **LNE:** Comprehensive entry on Ziemassvētki traditions, regional variants, and syncretic elements. [*Latvijas Nacionālā enciklopēdija*]
- **Šmits (Šmits):** Documents the Bluķis ceremony, Ķekatas mummer customs, and ancestor-feast practices. [Pēteris Šmits, *Latviešu mitoloģija*, 1926]

## Key Metadata
- **Region**: Latvia-wide
- **Tribe**: Latvians
- **Period**: Pre-Christian (winter solstice); current form blends with Christian Christmas from 15th century
- **Category**: Calendar Festival / Solar Rebirth / Ancestral Feast
- **Date**: December 21–26 (Winter Solstice to Christmas)
- **Symbol**: Bluķis log, Ķekatas mask, Candle, Extra place for dead, Sun-wheel
- **Deities invoked**: Dievs (cosmic renewal), Saule (returning sun), ancestral shades
- **Ritual actions**: Bluķis parade and burning, Ķekatas mummer visits, solstice feast, ancestor extra-place, sun-return songs
