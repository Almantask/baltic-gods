# Drebkulys

## Comprehensive Overview

Drebkulys (or Drebkulio) is a minor Lithuanian spirit personifying fever and the symptomatic "shakes" (chills/tremors). His name is derived from "drebėti" (to shake).

### English (EN)
**Drebkulys** is the spirit of the shivering fever. In Baltic folklore, major illnesses like fevers or the plague were often personified as supernatural beings. Drebkulys was a specialized spirit who "visited" people and caused them to shake uncontrollably. Offerings were made to appease him so that the fever would leave the patient. He represents the specialized nature of Baltic spirits, where each symptom was attributed to its own divine or demonic overseer.

### Lithuanian (LT)
**Drebkulys** lietuvių mitologijoje yra drugio, karščiavimo (krėtimo) ir nevalingo drebulio dvasia. Jo vardas kilo iš „drebėti“. Janas Lasickis nurodė, kad šiam dievui būdavo meldžiamasi norint atsikratyti karštinės (*pro febre*). Senovės lietuviai tikėjo, kad ligas siunčia rūsčios dvasios, kurias reikia permaldauti aukomis ar burtas. Drebkulys yra pavyzdys, kaip gamtos ir kūno procesai senovėje buvo sakralizuojami.

### Latvian (LV)
**Drebkulys** latviešu mitoloģijā nav ticis atsevišķi izdalīts, tā vietā to funkcijas veica dažādas **Karstnes** vai **Drugas** izpausmes. Tomēr lietuviešu tradīcijai ir lokālo sarakstu kontekstā Drebkulys reprezentē drudža un trīsas aizbildni, kura ziņā ir „nokratīt” cilvēku vai aiznest slimību prom, ja tas tiek permaldāts ar ticību un rituāliem.

## Primary Source Details

- **Łasicki (Łas):** Records *Drebkulum* as a deity of the shivering fever (*pro febre*), describing the supplications made for a cure.
- **Vėlius (Vel):** Analyzed the personification of diseases in Baltic folklore, linking Drebkulys to the broader complex of fever-maidens and plague-spirits.
- **VLE:** Confirms Drebkulys's status in the 16th-century pantheon lists as a specialized spirit of the shaking fever.

## Key Metadata
- **Category**: Health / Sickness Spirit
- **Symbol**: Trembling hand, Pale face, Shivering person
- **Function**: Causality and cure of the shaking fever
- **Etymology**: *drebėti (to shake)
