# Perkūnas / Pērkons / Perkūns

## Comprehensive Overview

Perkūnas (Lithuanian), Pērkons (Latvian), and Perkūns (Old Prussian) is the preeminent Baltic deity of thunder, lightning, storms, and the sky. As the second most powerful god after Dievas, he serves as the active protector of cosmic order, justice, and fertility.

### English (EN)
**Perkūnas** is the wielder of the celestial axe and hammer, riding a chariot through the heavens to hunt his chthonic rival, **Velnias**. He is the "purifier" of the earth; his first spring thunder awakens the soil and brings life to vegetation. He resides on high hills and is inseparable from the sacred **Oak** tree. In Latvian tradition, **Pērkons** is often seen with a large family, including sons who manifest different thunderous sounds and daughters who represent varying types of rain. He is a god of war but also a stern judge who punishes the wicked with his "thunder-bullets" (belemnite fossils).

### Lithuanian (LT)
**Perkūnas** yra galingiausias baltų atmosferos ir teisingumo dievas, dangaus kalvis, kurio kirvis ar kūjis skelia žaibus. Jis nuolatos kovoja su savo priešininku **Velniu**, kuris slepiasi po akmenimis ar medžiais, bandydamas pavogti derlių ar gyvulius. Perkūnas yra sakralizuoto **ąžuolo** globėjas; tikima, kad pirmasis pavasario griaustinis „atrakina“ žemę ir leidžia jai žaliuoti. Senovės lietuvių tikėjime jis buvo vaizduojamas kaip rūsčias barzdotas vyras, riedantis dangumi ožkų kinkiniu ar ugniniu vežimu.

### Latvian (LV)
**Pērkons** latviešu mitoloģijā ir debesu kalējs un taisnīguma sargs, kurš dzenā **Velnu** un attīra pasauli no ļaunuma. Viņš ir cieši saistīts ar auglību; dainās Pērkons bieži parādās kopā ar savu kuplo ģimeni — **Pērkona dēliem** un **meitām**, kas simbolizē dažādas dabas parādības. Pērkona simbols ir **Pērkona krusts** (ugunskrusts), kas sargā pret ļaunumu. Viņš jāj uz pelēka zirga vai brauc akmens ratiem, dārdinot debesis un metot zibens bultas, ko tauta dēvē par „Pērkona lodēm“.

## Primary Source Details

- **Łasicki (Łas):** Describes Perkūnas as the primary god of thunder and notes the "Perkūno oželis" (Thunder Goat) associations.
- **Stryjkowski (Str):** Mentions Perkūnas (Percunus) as the supreme god of the Lithuanians who was worshipped with eternal fire in groves.
- **Praetorius (Prae):** Details the iconographic representation of Perkūnas as a middle-aged, bearded man with a stern expression, associated with fire and oak.
- **Vėlius (Vel):** Analyzes the dualistic struggle between Perkūnas (the celestial sphere) and Velnias (the chthonic sphere), noting that Perkūnas is the guardian of social and cosmic stability.
- **Barons (Bar):** Cataloged thousands of Latvian *dainas* where Pērkons acts as a family man, a blacksmith, and a divine judge.
