# Dievas / Dievs / Dēiws

## Comprehensive Overview

Dievas (Lithuanian), Dievs (Latvian), and Dēiws (Old Prussian) is the supreme sky god of Baltic mythology. Reconstructed from the Proto-Indo-European root **\*deiwos** ("shining" or "celestial"), he represents the bright, luminous sky of the day and is the ultimate source of cosmic order, light, and life.

### English (EN)
**Dievas** is the supreme celestial patriarch, presiding over the world from the highest mountain of the sky. Unlike the more active and volatile Perkūnas, Dievas is often portrayed as a calm, benevolent, and wise gentleman-farmer who descends to earth disguised as an old man or traveler to test human virtue and hospitality. He is the guardian of boundaries, order, and social laws. In Latvian *dainas*, **Dievs** is a more intimate figure, often helping farmers in their fields, tending to crops, and ensuring the prosperity of the household. He is the father of the **Dieva dēli** (Sons of God), the celestial twins who rescue the Sun's daughters.

### Lithuanian (LT)
**Dievas** (kartais vadinamas *Aukščiausiuoju* ar *Praamžiumi*) yra vyriausiasis dangaus ir šviesos dievas, viso pasaulio tvarkos kūrėjas. Jis yra „šviečiantis“ dievas, susijęs su dienos šviesa ir giedru dangumi. Lietuvių sakmėse Dievas dažnai vaikšto žeme kaip senelis ar keliauninkas, stebėdamas, kaip žmonės laikosi teisingumo ir vaišingumo principų. Nors jis yra tolimas ir didingas, jo įtaka jaučiama per gamtos dėsnius ir moralinę tvarką. Jis yra tėvas, kurio vaikai – **Dievo sūneliai** – danguje rūpinasi Saulės žirgais.

### Latvian (LV)
**Dievs** latviešu mitoloģijā ir augstākā debesu būtne, taisnīguma, gaismas un kārtības avots. Viņš ir labvēlīgs un saudzīgs „debesu saimnieks”, kurš bieži nokāpj uz zemes, lai palīdzētu zemniekiem lauku darbos vai pārliecinātos par cilvēku dāsnumu. Dainās Dievs tiek attēlots tērpies pelēkos svārkos, jājot uz zirga vai braucot greznos ratos. Viņš ir **Dieva dēlu** tēvs un bieži pavada **Sauli** viņas debesu ceļojumā. Dievs dāvā cilvēkiem svētību, padomu un sargā mājas svētību.

## Primary Source Details

- **Stryjkowski (Str):** References the supreme god of the Lithuanians (often associated with the sky) as the source of all life and sovereignty.
- **Greimas (Grei):** Analyzes Dievas as the "sovereign" pole of the Baltic pantheon (using Dumézil's trifunctional framework), representing the magical-religious authority and the preservation of cosmic limits.
- **Barons (Bar):** Collected thousands of *dainas* where Dievs is a central, active character in rural life, often addressed with great affection and familiarity.
- **Biezais (Biez):** Conducted rigorous theological analysis of Dievs, emphasizing his roots in the Indo-European sky-father tradition and his distinctness from the Christian interpretation.
- **VLE:** Synthesizes the linguistic and mythological data, highlighting Dievas as the personification of the bright sky and the supreme moral judge.
