# Wurschkaito's Fire — Old Prussian Perpetual Fire-Keeping at Romowe

## Comprehensive Overview

**Wurschkaito** (also written *Worskaito*, *Worschkaite*) is the Old Prussian title for the fire-priests responsible for tending the eternal sacred flame at the grove of **Romowe**. Simon Grunau (*Preussische Chronik*, ca. 1529) identifies the *Worskaito* as the subordinate fire-keepers under the authority of the high priest *Kriwe*. While the **Romowe Fire Festival** entry documents the grove structure and the triad of deities, **Wurschkaito's Fire** focuses specifically on the role of the priestly fire-keeper — his duties, ritual authority, and the symbolic meaning of the perpetual flame he maintained.

### English (EN)
**Wurschkaito** (or *Worskaito*) is the Old Prussian priestly title for the fire-tender at the sacred grove of Romowe. According to Simon Grunau's *Preussische Chronik* (ca. 1529), the *Worskaito* held a specialised priestly role below the *Kriwe* (national high priest): his sole religious duty was to maintain the eternal fire burning before the image of **Perkuns** (thunder/fire) within the Romowe oak enclosure. The fire could never be permitted to go out — it was topped with dry oak and kept constantly alight. The *Worskaito* also performed specified purification rites before approaching the sacred flame: washing in cold water, wearing a specific white linen garment, and approaching the fire only from the east (the direction of sunrise). The flame itself was called *Swayxtix* in some variant transcriptions — identified with the sacred light principle, a deity or divine quality associated with the eternal fire. Additional fire-keepers (*Worskaito* could refer both to the individual and the category) were assigned to subsidiary sacred sites across the Prussian lands, each maintaining their own local flame. If the eternal fire at Romowe went out by accident, the *Kriwe* himself had to perform the relighting — which could only be accomplished by friction of dry wood, never by transferred fire from another source. The ritual extinguishing of the Romowe fire by the Teutonic Knights was therefore the most devastating symbolic act of the conquest — it could not be ritually remedied once the *Kriwe* himself was dead or in exile.

### Lithuanian (LT)
**Wurschkaito** — senprūsų žynio titulas, suteikiamas ugniasargiams Romowės šventnamyje. Simonas Grūnavus (*Preussische Chronik*, apie 1529) identifikuoja *Worskaito* kaip žemesnio rango kunigą po *Kriwe* (vyriausiasis kunigas), kurio vienintelė pareiga buvo prižiūrėti amžinąją ugnį prie **Perkūno** atvaizdo. Ugnis negalėjo užgesti — buvo papildoma sausu ąžuolo malku ir nuolat deganti. Worskaito atlikdavo apvalomąsias apeigas prieš prieidamas prie šventos ugnies: prausiosi šaltu vandeniu, vilkėjo baltą drobinį drabužį, artindavosi tik iš rytų. Ugnis buvo vadinama *Swayxtix* kai kuriose variantuose — susijusi su šventosios šviesos principu. Ugnis, kurią kryžiuočiai užgesino, negalėjo būti uždegta iš naujo po to, kai Kriwe buvo miręs.

### Latvian (LV)
**Wurschkaito** ir Senprūšu priestera tituls uguns sargam Romowes svētajā birzī. Sīmans Grunaus (*Preussische Chronik*, ap 1529) identificē *Worskaito* kā zemāka ranga priesteri zem *Kriwe* (nacionālais augstpriesters), kura vienīgais pienākums bija uzturēt mūžīgo uguni pie **Perkuna** attēla. Uguns nekad nedrīkstēja nodzist — to papildināja ar sausu ozola malku un turēja nepārtraukti degošu. Worskaito veica šķīstīšanās rituālus pirms tuvoties svētajai ugunij: mazgājās aukstā ūdenī, valkāja baltu linu apģērbu, tuvojās tikai no austrumiem. Uguns tika saukta *Swayxtix* dažās versijās — saistīta ar svētā gaismas principa dievību. Teutoniešu ordeņa simboliskais nodarījums bija Romowes uguns dzēšana, ko vairs nevarēja rituāli atlīdzināt pēc Kriwe nāves.

## Primary Source Details

- **Simon Grunau (Grunau):** Primary source documenting the *Worskaito* title, duties, purification rites, and the *Swayxtix* fire-name. [Simon Grunau, *Preussische Chronik*, ca. 1529; Book I]
- **Peter of Dusburg (Dur):** Foundation description of the Romowe eternal fire and Kriwe priestly hierarchy within which the *Worskaito* operated. [Peter of Dusburg, *Chronicon terrae Prussiae*, 1326; Book III, Chapter 5]
- **Einhorn (Ein):** Comparative documentation of fire-keeper practices in Baltic territories. [Paul Einhorn, *Historia Lettica*, 1649]

## Key Metadata
- **Region**: Nadruvia, Old Prussia (Romowe grove); subsidiary fire sites across Prussian lands
- **Tribe**: Old Prussians (priestly class)
- **Period**: Pre-Christian; documented by Grunau ca. 1529 from older chronicles; practice active through c. 1350–1400 CE (Teutonic conquest)
- **Category**: Sacred Office / Perpetual Fire / Priestly Ritual
- **Date**: Continuous — daily maintenance with no interruption permitted
- **Symbol**: Eternal flame (*Swayxtix*), Dry oak fuel, White linen vestment, Eastern approach, Kriwe's staff, Romowe oak-enclosure
- **Deities invoked**: Perkuns (thunder/fire — the fire burns before his image), Swayxtix (sacred light/fire deity or divine quality)
- **Ritual actions**: Daily fire-stoking with dry oak, purification washing, white-garment dressing, east-facing approach, fire-relighting by friction only
- **References**:
    - "Grunau, Simon: Preussische Chronik (c. 1529)"
    - "Peter of Dusburg: Chronicon terrae Prussiae (1326)"
    - "Einhorn, Paul: Historia Lettica (1649)"
