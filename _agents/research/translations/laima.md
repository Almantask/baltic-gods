# Laima / Laimė

## Comprehensive Overview

Laima (Latvian and Lithuanian) or Laimė (Lithuanian variant) is the goddess of fate, destiny, luck, and birth in Baltic mythology. As the weaver of human life, she determines the lifespan, fortune, and major milestones of every individual from the moment of their birth until their death.

### English (EN)
**Laima** is the primary arbiter of fate, balancing the threads of life with wisdom and detachment. She is most famously associated with childbirth, appearing at the threshold of the home (or the bathhouse) to proclaim a newborn's destiny. Her decisions are often considered final, though she can be invoked for blessings in marriage, fertility, and general well-being. Laima is deeply linked to the **Linden tree** and is sometimes accompanied by her two sisters in Latvian tradition, **Dēkla** and **Kārta**, who assist in measuring and cutting the thread of life.

### Lithuanian (LT)
**Laima** (dažnai tapatinama su *Laimė*) yra likimo, lemties ir gimimo deivė. Ji yra ta, kuri „nulemia“ žmogaus gyvenimo kelią, jo sėkmę ar nesėkmę. Lietuvių tikėjime Laima dažnai pasirodo kaip moteris, sėdinti už krosnies ar prie gimdyvės lovos, ir savo žodžiu užantspauduojanti vaiko ateitį. Ji taip pat yra susijusi su gegute, kuri savo kukavimu pranašauja likusius gyvenimo metus. Laima globoja moteris, šeimos laimę ir padeda surasti tinkamą porą santuokai.

### Latvian (LV)
**Laima** latviešu mitoloģijā un dainās ir vissvarīgākā likteņa dievība, kura nosaka cilvēka mūža garumu un laimi. Viņa ir klātesoša katrā nozīmīgā dzīves brīdī, īpaši dzemdībās, kuras vēsturiski notika pirtī. Laima dainās tiek uzrunāta ar lielu cieņu kā „Laimiņa”. Viņa bieži darbojas kopā ar **Dēklu** un **Kārtu**, veidojot likteņa trīsvienību. Laimas simbols ir **liepa**, un viņas ziņā ir svētīt gan cilvēkus, gan viņu sētu un lopus, dāvājot „baltas dienas”.

## Primary Source Details

- **Łasicki (Łas):** Records Laima as a goddess of fortune and notes her importance in the household cults of the 16th century.
- **Barons (Bar):** Cataloged thousands of *dainas* where Laima is the most frequently mentioned female deity, acting as a personal guardian and a divine witness to life's struggles.
- **Vėlius (Vel2):** Explores the structural role of Laima in the "destiny-determination" complex of Baltic mythology, linking her to ancient Indo-European weavers of fate.
- **Biezais (Biez):** Provided extensive research into the "Fate triad" in Latvian mythology, distinguishing the roles of Laima from her sisters Dēkla and Kārta.
- **VLE:** Summarizes Laima's role as the personification of luck and destiny, confirming her central status in the Baltic spiritual hierarchy.
