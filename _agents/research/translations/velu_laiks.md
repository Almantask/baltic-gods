# Veļu laiks — Latvian Time of the Dead

## Comprehensive Overview

**Veļu laiks** ("Time of the Shades/Dead") is the Latvian autumn period — roughly the last two weeks of October and the first two weeks of November — when ancestral spirits (*veļi*) return to the living world and are formally hosted, fed, and then ceremonially released. It is the Latvian equivalent of Lithuanian **Vėlinės** and the broader pan-Baltic ancestor festival complex. The presiding deity is **Veļu māte** (Mother of the Dead), who governs the underworld and regulates the movement of souls.

### English (EN)
**Veļu laiks** is the Latvian "Season of the Dead," a two-to-four week period in late autumn when the boundary between the living world and the realm of the dead (*Veļu valsts*) becomes thin. The ancestral shades (*veļi*) roam freely, revisiting their former homes, fields, animals, and family members. To honour them properly, families prepared special foods and set them out on the doorstep overnight; the bathhouse (*pirts*) was heated for the dead to cleanse themselves; and bonfires were lit at crossroads or field-edges to guide the wandering spirits and warn off malicious ones. The festival includes two distinctly different modes: the hospitable reception of one's own ancestral dead (who are welcomed, fed, and spoken to), and the warding-off of stranger spirits or potentially hostile revenant forces. **Veļu māte** (Mother of the Dead) is the gatekeeper who opens and closes the passage for the veļi. She is respectfully addressed with offerings of food and candles. At the end of Veļu laiks, the ancestral spirits are formally "sent back" — typically by speaking a farewell formula and sweeping the threshold.

### Lithuanian (LT)
**Veļu laiks** (latv., liet. *Vėlinės*) — latvių mirusiųjų grįžimo laikotarpis rugsėjo–lapkričio sandūroje. Vėlės (veļi) vaikšto po savo buvusius namus ir laukus. Šeimos ruošia valgius ant slenksčio, kūrena pirtį mirusiems, dega laužus pakelėse. **Veļu māte** (mirusiųjų motina) yra laiko šeimininkė: ji leidžia veļus eiti ir kviečia juos sugrįžti. Šventės pabaigoje veļi oficialiai „išleidžiami" atgal, kalbant atsisveikinimo formulę ir šluojant slenkstį. Tai atitinka lietuviškas Vėlinių apeigos struktūrą.

### Latvian (LV)
**Veļu laiks** ir viens no svarīgākajiem latviešu gadskārtu cikla posmiem. Tā ilgums — apmēram divas četras nedēļas rudenī — atšķir to no vienas dienas svētkiem un interpretē to kā periodu, nevis vienu pasākumu. Veļi ir gan labvēlīgi, gan potenciāli bīstami: tie ir senči, kas vēl nav atpūtušies, vai svešinieki, kam nav savas ģimenes. Abi jāapkalpo pareizi. Veļu māte ir Nāves pasaules valdniece, kura uzrauga šo procesu. Pirts kūrināšana mirušajiem, upuru ēdienu atstāšana pie durvīm un aizdegtas sveces kapus apsenam ir galvenie rituālie elementi, kas saglabājušies latviešu folkloras pierakstos.

## Primary Source Details

- **Barons (Bar):** Numerous *dainas* specifically for Veļu laiks, including farewell songs for the dead and Veļu māte invocations. [Krišjānis Barons, *Latvju dainas*, 1894–1915]
- **Šmits (Šmits):** Detailed documentation of Veļu laiks customs — food-offerings, bathhouse heating, crossroads bonfires, and the formal release ceremony. [Pēteris Šmits, *Latviešu tautas ticējumi jeb pasakas*, 15 volumes, 1925–37]
- **Biezais (Biez):** Theological analysis of Veļu māte's role in Latvian eschatology. [Haralds Biezais, *Die Hauptgöttinnen der alten Letten*, 1955]

## Key Metadata
- **Region**: Latvia-wide
- **Tribe**: Latvians
- **Period**: Proto-Baltic; active folk tradition; partially merged with All Souls (Nov 2)
- **Category**: Seasonal Period / Ancestor Cult Ritual
- **Date**: Late October – early November (approx. 2–4 weeks)
- **Symbol**: Threshold offerings, Bathhouse fire, Crossroads bonfire, Candle, Farewell broom
- **Deity**: Veļu māte (Mother of the Dead/Shades)
- **Ritual actions**: Food-setting at doorstep, bathhouse heating for dead, crossroads bonfire, ancestral meals, formal dismissal ceremony
- **References**:
    - "Barons, Krišjānis: Latvju dainas (1894–1915)"
    - "Šmits, Pēteris: Latviešu mitoloģija (1918)"
