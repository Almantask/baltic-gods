# Perkunos Festival — Old Prussian Thunder-Fire and Goat Sacrifice

## Comprehensive Overview

**Perkunos Festival** refers to the Old Prussian rituals performed at sacred oak groves in honour of **Perkuns** (the thunder deity, cognate with Lithuanian *Perkūnas*). These rites — including the lighting of sacred fire, goat sacrifice, communal beer drinking, and prayers at oak trees during thunderstorms — are documented primarily by Peter of Dusburg (*Chronicon terrae Prussiae*, 1326) and are among the earliest first-person chronicle attestations of Baltic religious practice.

### English (EN)
The **Perkunos Festival** is the collective term for the Old Prussian ritual complex honouring **Perkuns**, the thunder deity. According to Peter of Dusburg (1326), the Prussians maintained a fire (*ignis perpetuus*) at sacred oak groves in Perkuns's honour as part of the Romowe sacred complex — one flame specifically designated as Perkuns's portion of the triad fire. Independently, when a thunderstorm approached, the Prussians would gather at the nearest sacred oak, light a fire from the lightning-struck bark if available, sacrifice a goat (*capra*), and pour communal beer while invoking the thunder god to send rain to the crops and withhold destructive bolts from the cattle and farmsteads. The goat, as an animal associated with the sky and clouds across Indo-European traditions, was Perkuns's preferred sacrifice. The ashes of the sacrificed goat were scattered on fields to fertilise them. Perkuns was also invoked by smiths, as lightning striking an oak tree was understood as Perkuns's hammer blow. Sacred oaks struck by lightning were thereafter permanently dedicated to Perkuns — an altar-clearing was made around them, and they could not be felled without ritual consequence. The Teutonic Knights' policy of felling sacred oaks was explicitly viewed as desecration of Perkuns's domain.

### Lithuanian (LT)
**Perkunos šventė** — senprūsų perkūno dievo (*Perkuns*) garbinimo ritualų kompleksas prie šventų ąžuolų. Petras iš Dusburgo (1326) aprašo amžiną ugnį Perkūno garbei bei ožio auką (*capra*) perkūnijos metu. Bendruomenė rinkdavosi prie artimiausio šventojo ąžuolo, uždegdavo ugnį, aukodavo ožį ir liedavo alų prašydami lietaus pasėliams. Perkūno varu numušto žaibo nutrenktų ąžuolų pelenai buvo beriami ant laukų kaip trąša. Žaibo numušti ąžuolai tapdavo amžininkais altoriais.

### Latvian (LV)
**Perkunos svētks** ir Senprūšu pērkona dieva **Perkuns** godāšanas rituālu kopums pie svētajiem ozoliem. Petrs no Dusburgas (1326) dokumentē mūžīgo uguni Perkuna godam un kazas (*capra*) upurēšanu pērkona laikā. Kopiena pulcējās pie tuvākā svētā ozola, iededzināja uguni, upurēja kazu un lēja kopīgo alu, lūdzot lietu laukiem un draudzīgus pērkona gāzienus. Perkuna uztriekts ozols kļuva par mūžīgu altāri — ap to tika izveidota klajuma josla un bez rituāla nedrīkstēja nocirst.

## Primary Source Details

- **Peter of Dusburg (Dur):** Primary witness documentation of oak-grove fire maintenance for Perkuns and goat sacrifice. [Peter of Dusburg, *Chronicon terrae Prussiae*, 1326; Book III]
- **Simon Grunau (Grunau):** Extended notes on the Romowe fire's three-section division and Perkuns's fire. [Simon Grunau, *Preussische Chronik*, ca. 1529]
- **Fabricius (Fab):** Mentions oak-cult practices in Old Prussian territories. [Dionysius Fabricius, *Livonicae Historiae Compendiosa Series*, 1611]

## Key Metadata
- **Region**: Old Prussia — particularly Nadruvia, Sambia, and Pomerania; sacred oak groves throughout
- **Tribe**: Old Prussians (Sudovians, Galindians, Sambians, Nadruvians)
- **Period**: Pre-Christian; documented c. 1326 CE; practices continue regionally until 15th century CE under Teutonic pressure
- **Category**: Storm Ritual / Sacrifice / Agricultural Prayer
- **Date**: Performed during or after thunderstorms; also part of Romowe continuous fire maintenance
- **Symbol**: Sacred oak, Eternal fire (Perkuns portion), Goat (*capra*), Beer (*alus*), Lightning-struck bark
- **Deities invoked**: Perkuns (thunder/storm, Old Prussian cognate of Lithuanian Perkūnas)
- **Ritual actions**: Fire-lighting at oak, goat sacrifice, communal beer pouring, field-ash fertilisation, dedication of lightning-struck oaks
- **References**:
    - "Peter of Dusburg: Chronicon terrae Prussiae (1326)"
    - "Grunau, Simon: Preussische Chronik (c. 1529)"
    - "Fabricius, Dionysius: Livonicae Historiae Compendiosa Series (1611)"
