# Miķeļi — Latvian Autumn Harvest-Close Festival

## Comprehensive Overview

**Miķeļi** (September 29, Michaelmas) is the Latvian autumn festival marking the closing of the outdoor harvest season, the settling of farm accounts, and the transition into the indoor winter work period. While its name is borrowed from St. Michael the Archangel in Christian overlay, the ritual content of Miķeļi is rooted in pre-Christian autumn thanksgiving and agricultural cycle-closing.

### English (EN)
**Miķeļi** is the Latvian autumn-close festival, observed on September 29. It represents the formal end of the outdoor harvest: all grain must be safely stored by Miķeļi, all debts from the year's farm-labour settled, and all farmhands paid. Any grain left in the fields after Miķeļi is said to belong to the field spirits or the dead. The main activities are a communal harvest-thanksgiving meal (*miķeļu mielasts*), the drinking of the first beer brewed from the new harvest's grain, and the formal dismissal of summer farmhands. The farmstead's harvest wreath (*vainags*) from the grain-cutting season is hung above the main door or the granary as a protective talisman. The day is associated with women beginning the major autumn indoor work: textile production (spinning, weaving, carding) that would occupy the household through winter. The patron supernatural forces are **Jumis** (the double-grain spirit of abundance) being carried safely into the granary, and the general agricultural mātes (field, grain, and barn mothers) who are thanked for their summer guardianship.

### Lithuanian (LT)
**Miķeļi** (latv., liet. analogas *Žolinė / Dožinktuvės*) — latvių rudens harvesto uždarymo šventė. Rugsėjo 29 d. baigiamos visos lauko darbai, laukai tuščios, samdiniai apmokėti. Rugių alaus pirmas bidon išgertas. Metų javų vainikamas – lauko nupjovimo pabaigos simbolis – pakabinamas virš aruodo durų. Pradedamas žieminis audimo ir verpimo sezonas. Šventė analogiška lietuviškai **Dožinktuvės** ir **Žolinė** – abu žymi žemės darbo metinę pabaigą.

### Latvian (LV)
**Miķeļi** ir viens no galvenajiem latviešu gadskārtas svētkiem — rudens saulgriežu atbalsis, kas iezīmē lauku darbu beigas. Miķeļu mielastā tiek novadīts pirmais alus no jaunās labības; ražas vainags iekar virs klēts durvīm. Saimnieki norēķinās ar kalpiem. Sievietes sāk rudens-ziemas tekstildarbus. Juma nešana labības klētī un mātes (lauka māte, rijas māte, graudu māte) pateicināšana ir garīgais svētku centrs. Miķeļi ir Latvijas lauksaimnieciskā gada slēgums jeb noslēdzošais akts pirms Veļu laika un Ziemassvētku perioda.

## Primary Source Details

- **Barons (Bar):** Autumn harvest-close *dainas* including thanksgiving verses for Miķeļi. [Krišjānis Barons, *Latvju dainas*, 1894–1915]
- **Šmits (Šmits):** Documents the Miķeļu mielasts feast, farmhand payment customs, and the beginning of indoor textile work. [Pēteris Šmits, *Latviešu mitoloģija*, 1926]

## Key Metadata
- **Region**: Latvia-wide
- **Tribe**: Latvians
- **Period**: Pre-Christian harvest-close; aligned with Michaelmas from medieval period
- **Category**: Calendar Festival / Agricultural / Accounting Ritual
- **Date**: September 29 (Michaelmas)
- **Symbol**: Harvest wreath (*vainags*), Beer barrel (from new grain), Granary, Spindle
- **Deities invoked**: Jumis (double-grain abundance), grain and field mātes
- **Ritual actions**: First new-grain beer tasting, harvest-wreath hanging, farmhand payment, textile-work opening
- **References**:
    - "Barons, Krišjānis: Latvju dainas (1894–1915)"
