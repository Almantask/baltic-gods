# Žemaitija Star Field / Žemaitijos Žvaigždžių Laukas / Žemaitijas Zvaigžņu Lauks

## Comprehensive Overview

The **Žemaitija Star Field** is a forest clearing in the Samogitia region of western Lithuania where pine trunks serve as posts for star charts — hand-drawn maps of constellation positions — updated by local sky-watchers on every clear night. The site is dedicated to **Dievas**, the sky father, in his Lithuanian expression as the ordered firmament whose structure can be read and followed by attentive humans. Samogitia's flat landscape and relatively northern latitude give it dark, wide-horizon skies that made constellation observation practically useful for navigation, agriculture, and festival timing. The practice of marking resin drops on star-chart parchment as "new stars" reflects a cosmology in which the sky was understood as a living document continuously authored by the deity, not a static fixed map.

### English (EN)
**Dievas** in Lithuanian tradition was not merely the sky as container but the sky as order — the principle that created the celestial pattern that all living things move within. His cult was intellectual and devotional rather than dramatic: it was expressed through careful observation, record-keeping, and the recitation of ancestral lines that located living people within the cosmic family. At the Žemaitija clearing, these practices converged. Star charts pinned to pine trunks with carved wooden stakes were updated each clear night: new observation marks were added and previous marks compared against the current positions to track seasonal drift. Pine resin drips falling randomly onto the parchment were incorporated as "new stars" — accepted rather than discarded, because randomness within the divine's creation was also meaningful. At midnight, observers lay on the clearing's moss and whispered names of ancestors to each constellation; they believed the sky repeated one of the names in the rustle of branches just after midnight — a single word spoken by Dievas confirming that the named ancestor was located and held within the celestial order. This practice is related to the broader Baltic veneration of ancestors as celestial bodies (*žvaigždės*, stars) that have taken their place in the sky family.

### Lithuanian (LT)
**Dievas** lietuvių tradicijoje buvo ne tik dangus kaip konteineris, bet dangus kaip tvarka — principas, sukūręs dangaus raštą, kuriame juda visi gyvi dalykai. Jo kultas buvo intelektualus ir meditacinis, o ne dramatiškas: jis buvo išreikštas per kruopštų stebėjimą, įrašų tvarkymą ir protėvių linijų kartojimą, kuris išdėstė gyvus žmones kosminiame šeimoje. Žemaitijos aikštelėje šios praktikos susijungė. Žvaigždžių žemėlapiai, pritvirtinti prie pušų kamienų drožtais mediniais kuolais, buvo atnaujinami kiekvieną aiškią naktį. Pušų dervos lašai, atsitiktinai krintantys ant pergamento, buvo įtraukiami kaip „naujos žvaigždės" — priimami, o ne atmetami, nes atsitiktinumas dieviškojo kūrinyje irgi buvo prasmingas. Vidurnaktį stebėtojai gulėdavo aikštelės samanose ir šnibždėdavo protėvių vardus kiekvienam žvaigždynui.

### Latvian (LV)
**Dievs** lietuviešu tradīcijā nebija tikai debesis kā konteiners, bet debesis kā kārtība — princips, kas radīja debesu rakstu, kurā pārvietojas visi dzīvie radījumi. Viņa kults bija intelektuāls un uzticīgs, nevis dramatisks: to izteica caur rūpīgu novērojumu, uzskaiti un senču līniju recitēšanu, kas lokalizēja dzīvus cilvēkus kosmiskajā saimē. Žemaitijas klajumā šīs prakses safūzionēja. Zvaigžņu kartes, piestiprinātas pie priežu stumbriem ar grebtiem koka mietu, tika atjauninātas katru skaidru nakti. Priedes sveķu pilieni, kas nejauši krit uz pergamenta, tika iekļauti kā "jaunas zvaigznes" — pieņemti, nevis izmesti, jo nejaušība dievišķā radījumā arī bija nozīmīga. Pusnaktī novērotāji gulēja uz klajuma sūnām un čukstēja senču vārdus katram zvaigznājam.

## Primary Source Details

- **Stryjkowski (Str):** Documents Lithuanian star-reading traditions and the Samogitian veneration of sky-order as an expression of the supreme deity. [Maciej Stryjkowski, *Kronika*, 1582]
- **Narbutas (Nar):** Lithuanian historical records including accounts of Samogitian star-observatory clearings and their connection to ancestral veneration. [Teodoras Narbutas, *Lietuvių tautos istorija*, 9 vols, 1835–41]
- **Vėlius (Vel2):** Analysis of Baltic cosmological worldview, including the sky-as-order principle and ancestor-star identification. [Norbertas Vėlius, *Senovės baltų pasaulėžiūra*, 1983]
- **Gimbutas (Gimb):** Documents the Baltic concept of stars as ancestor-souls and the celestial family structure of the sky. [Marija Gimbutas, *The Balts*, 1963]
- **Beresnevičius (Ber):** Analyses Dievas's expression in Samogitian sky ritual and the practice of night-vigil star-mapping. [Gintaras Beresnevičius, *Lietuvių religija ir mitologija*, 2004]
