# Dožinktuvės — Lithuanian Harvest-End Festival

## Comprehensive Overview

**Dožinktuvės** (also *dožinki* or *dožinkos*; related to *dožinkti* — to finish reaping) is the Lithuanian harvest-end festival, celebrated when the last grain was cut from the fields, typically in August or September. It centres on the ceremony of the **last sheaf** (*paskutinis pėdas* or **Jievaras**), the formal closing of the grain harvest, communal thanksgiving, and the presentation of a harvest wreath to the landowner or household head.

### English (EN)
**Dožinktuvės** is the Lithuanian harvest-end festival — the formal closure of the grain-cutting season. When the last stalks of rye or wheat were cut, the reapers braided them into a special decorative sheaf (*Jievaras*) or a large harvest wreath (*vainius* or *vainikas*) woven with grain-heads, wild flowers, and ribbons. This wreath was carried in ceremonial procession back to the farmstead by the lead harvester — often a young woman — and presented with songs to the head of the household. The landowner accepted the wreath and hung it in the house for the year, where it would protect the family and guarantee a good harvest the following year. A communal feast followed, with freshly baked bread (*šviežia duona*), beer (*alus*), and roasted meats. **Žemyna** (the Earth Goddess) received the first and last toasts. **Gabjauja** (the Grain Barn Goddess) was thanked for keeping the stored grain safe. The Jievaras sheaf itself was sometimes kept until the next spring plowing and then buried in the first furrow, closing the agricultural cycle. Songs of the Dožinktuvės praised the earth's generosity, beseeched Laima's continued favour, and acknowledged the end of the summer sun's power.

### Lithuanian (LT)
**Dožinktuvės** – lietuvių derliaus pabaigos šventė, švenčiama baigus pjūtį. Paskutiniai rugiai ar kviečiai pinami į puošnų pėdą (**Jievarą**) ar vainikus. Vainius procesijoje nešamas šeimininkui ir įteikiamas su dainomis. Šeimininkė ar šeimininkas priima jį ir kabo namuose – apsauga visus metus. Tada rengiamas bendras puota: šviežia duona, alus, kepta mėsa. **Žemynai** aukojamas pirmas ir paskutinis puodelis. **Gabjaujai** dėkojama už laikytą grūdą. Jievaras kartais laikomas iki pavasario ir užaramais pirmame vage – uždaro žemdirbystės ciklą. Dainuojamos padėkos dainos žemei ir **Laimai**.

### Latvian (LV)
**Apjumības** (latviešu ražas noslēguma paralēle) ir analogas beigu svētki, kur pēdējās labības kūlīšs (*Jumis*) tiek atnests svinīgi. **Jumis** — divkāršā graudu vārpa — ir latviešu pēdējā kūlīša svētais tēls, kā Jievaras lietuviešu tradīcijā. Abos gadījumos pēdējo graudu novākšana ir sakrāls slēgšanas akts, kur zemnieks pateicas zemei par dāvāto ražu un aicina to atjaunoties nākamgad.

- **Folk tradition:** Harvest-song collections with Dožinktuvės-specific ceremonial texts preserved in regional ethnographic archives.

## Key Metadata
- **Region**: Lithuania-wide
- **Tribe**: Lithuanians
- **Period**: Proto-Baltic; attested in folk custom from 17th century; parallel to pan-European last-sheaf traditions
- **Category**: Calendar Festival / Agricultural / Thanksgiving Ritual
- **Date**: August–September (when grain cutting is complete)
- **Symbol**: Last sheaf (*Jievaras*), Harvest wreath (*vainikas*), Fresh bread, Beer barrel
- **Deities invoked**: Žemyna (earth), Gabjauja (barn), Jievaras (last-sheaf spirit), Laima (fate)
- **Ritual actions**: Last-sheaf braiding, harvest wreath procession, landowner presentation, communal feast, spring re-burial of Jievaras
