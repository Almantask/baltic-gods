# Lieldienas — Latvian Spring Equinox Festival

## Comprehensive Overview

**Lieldienas** ("Great Days") is the Latvian spring equinox festival, observed around March 21 and partially aligned with Christian Easter. It is the Latvian equivalent of Lithuanian **Pavasario lygiadienis**, centred on the return of the sun, decorated eggs (*olas* / *pūķu olas*), swinging on outdoor swings, and the formal awakening of the earth's productive forces.

### English (EN)
**Lieldienas** is the Latvian spring festival marking the balance-point of the solar year when day and night are equal — and then day begins to prevail. Its central ritual objects are **decorated eggs** (*Lieldienu olas*): eggs are dyed using natural materials (onion peels, birch leaves, beet juice) or decorated with wax-resist patterns. The egg represents the cosmic creative potential — the world egg from which spring is hatched. **Egg-rolling** (*ripināšana*) down gentle hillsides is practiced as sympathetic magic to encourage the sun's rising arc. **Egg-fighting** (*čaukstināšana* — cracking two eggs together; the one whose shell survives intact wins) is both a game and a solar omen-reading act. Young people swing on large outdoor swings, seen throughout Baltic tradition as a way to help the sun rise higher. Special Lieldienas foods include coloured eggs, pork, and the first spring cheeses. **Saule** (the Sun Goddess) is the central divine figure returning from winter; **Māra** (as earth-spring) is invoked to open the soil; and **Laima** is consulted through the egg patterns and swing-flights as a fate-oracle.

### Lithuanian (LT)
**Lieldienas** (latv., liet. *Velykos* / *Pavasario lygiadienis*) — latvių pavasario šventė, švenčiama apie kovo 21 d. Pagrindiniai ritualai: **kiaušinių dažymas** (svogūnų žievelėmis, beržo lapais), **kiaušinių ridimas** ant kalvos (saulės iškilimo magija) ir **kiaušinių mušimas** (*čaušķināšana*). Sūpynių sūpimasis (saulės kylimo magija). Šventės globėjai: **Saulė** (grįžtanti saulė), **Māra** (žemė), **Laima** (likimas). Tai tiesioginis latvių ir lietuvių pavasario sugrįžimo šventės analogas.

### Latvian (LV)
**Lieldienas** ir otrs lielākais latvju gadskārtu svētks pēc Jāņiem. Olu krāsošana un ripināšana ir svētku kodols: ola simbolizē saules dzeltenumu, auglību un atdzimšanu. Šūpoles (*šūpoles*) ārā tiek karinātas jau marta beigās, un Lieldienu dienā visi — bērni, jaunieši, pieaugušie — šūpojas, lai palīdzētu saulei celties augstāk. Tradīcijā saglabājusies arī **laimes lasīšana** no olas formas un dārgmeta krāsas: zaļa kārtainā ola solīja labu ražu, sarkana — laimi mīlestībā. Lieldienas ir arī mirušo piemiņas diena — kapus apmeklē agrā rītā pirms saullēkta.

## Key Metadata
- **Region**: Latvia-wide
- **Tribe**: Latvians
- **Period**: Pre-Christian (spring equinox); syncretic with Christian Easter from 15th century
- **Category**: Calendar Festival / Solar Ritual / Fertility Magic
- **Date**: Around March 21 (Spring Equinox), aligned with Easter
- **Symbol**: Decorated egg (*Lieldienu ola*), Swing (*šūpoles*), Onion-peel dye, Sun-wheel pattern
- **Deities invoked**: Saule (returning sun), Māra (earth-spring), Laima (fate)
- **Ritual actions**: Egg-decoration, egg-rolling, egg-fighting, swinging, dawn grave-visit, first spring feast
