# Māras diena — Latvian Day of Māra / Earth-Opening Day

## Comprehensive Overview

**Māras diena** ("Day of Māra") is the Latvian spring observance on March 25, marking the moment when the earth (*Māra's body*) officially opens and becomes receptive to seeds, when cattle are returned to pasture, and when the great goddess **Māra** — who governs the material world, cattle, and the body — stirs from her winter state. It is associated in Christian overlay with the Annunciation of the Virgin Mary (March 25), but its mythological core is pre-Christian and centred on the agricultural and zoological implications of Māra's awakening.

### English (EN)
**Māras diena** (March 25) is the day in the Latvian calendar when **Māra** formally awakens and the earth opens its body to receive the year's first agricultural work. Māra governs all material life: bodies, cattle, milk, soil, and the substance of the living world. Her day in late March is the threshold between the frozen, closed earth of winter and the open, receptive earth of spring. Cattle were traditionally allowed outside for their first spring grazing on Māras diena; the first milk of the season received special attention — it was boiled or offered to the threshold as a gift to Māra. Women performed ritual acts at wells and springs, which were seen as Māra's eyes in the earth, thanking her for the winter's survival and inviting her summer fertility. The threshold of the house (Māra's dwelling zone in domestic space) was swept and ritually cleansed. Songs and prayers addressed Māra directly, acknowledging her dual role as life-sustainer (cattle-protector and milk-giver) and death-mediator (she receives the body at death, as Laima assigns the fate). March 25 was also the day to begin preparing seeds: this was done with prayers to Māra that the seeds would be generative and the soil receptive.

### Lithuanian (LT)
**Māras diena** (latv., liet. *Žemynos dienа* – analogiškas žemės atbudimo ritualas) — latvių kovo 25 d. **Māra** (didžioji deivė, materijos pasaulio valdovė) atsibunda, žemė atsidaro, galvijai pirmą kartą išleidžiami. Iš pradžių pienas boilinamas ar aukojamas slenksčiui – dovanota **Mārai**. Moterys atlieka ritualus prie šaltinių (Māros akies žemėje). Sėklos ruošiamos su maldų formulėmis. Artimiausia lietuviška paralelė – **Žemynos** pagerbimas prieš pirmąjį ariną.

### Latvian (LV)
**Māras diena** ir viens no svarīgākajiem agrāwiem latviešu pavasara rituāliem. Māras attīšanās no ziemas nosaka, kad zeme kļūst auglīga un lopu ganīšana var sākties. Govju un aitu pirmreizēja izvešana ganībās šajā dienā ir svēts akts, ko pavada dziesmas Mārai. Kā piena dieviete, Māra nodrošina, ka pirmā pavasara piens ir bagātīgs un veselīgs. Avoti un akas tiek apmeklēti kā Māras izpausmes vietās zemes virspusē. Māras galvenā loma ir saistīt zemi, miesu un materiālo pasauli ar garīgo izcelsmi — caur pienu, graudu un dzīvnieku veselību.

## Primary Source Details

- **Barons (Bar):** Spring *dainas* specifically marking March 25 as Māra's seasonal awakening. [Krišjānis Barons, *Latvju dainas*, 1894–1915]
- **LNE:** Article on Māras diena traditions and their relationship to the Annunciation and pre-Christian spring rites. [*Latvijas Nacionālā enciklopēdija*]
- **Biezais (Biez):** Theological analysis of Māra's role in the agricultural calendar, including March awakening traditions. [Haralds Biezais, *Die Hauptgöttinnen der alten Letten*, 1955]
- **Zepa (Zep):** Documents Māra's material-world aspects that give Māras diena its specific focus on cattle and milk. [Baiba Zepa, *Māras zeme*, 2000]

## Key Metadata
- **Region**: Latvia-wide
- **Tribe**: Latvians
- **Period**: Pre-Christian; syncretic with Christian Annunciation (March 25) from 15th century
- **Category**: Calendar Festival / Agricultural / Spring-Opening Ritual
- **Date**: March 25 (Annunciation / Māra's awakening)
- **Symbol**: First milk, Spring well, First cattle outdoors, Threshold sweep, Seed preparation
- **Deity**: Māra (material world, cattle, milk, earth-body)
- **Ritual actions**: First cattle-grazing, milk offering to threshold, well-visit rites, seed preparation with prayer, threshold cleansing
