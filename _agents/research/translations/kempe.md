# Kempe

## Comprehensive Overview

Kempe (recorded by Łasicki as *Kempe pro scarabaeis*) is a minor Lithuanian (Samogitian) spirit or deity associated with beetles, particularly cockchafers.

### English (EN)
**Kempe** is a nature deity or spirit whose primary domain is beetles. In the Baltic worldview, even the smallest natural domains like beetles or specific types of insects were believed to have their own divine overseer. Kempe was especially invoked to ensure that the beetles did not damage crops or were kept in a healthy balance within the farmstead. He represents the specialized nature of Baltic spirits, where each creature, no matter how small, was attributed with its own divine guardian.

### Lithuanian (LT)
**Kempe** lietuvių mitologijoje yra vabalų, o ypač karkvabalių, dievas ar deivė. Jį mini Janas Lasickis savo dievų sąraše, nurodydamas, kad šiam dievui būdavo meldžiamasi norint atsikratyti ar pagerbti vabalus (*pro scarabaeis*). Kempe simbolizuoja žemdirbio triūsą atrenkant geriausią sėklą ir sargyba, užtikrinanti augalijai bei kitiems vabalams sveikatą ir gamtos ryšį.

### Latvian (LV)
**Kempe** latviešu mitoloģijā nav ticis atsevišķi izdalīts, tā vietā to funkcijas veica dažādas **Vaboles mātės** vai **Zemes mātest** izpausmes. Tomēr lietuviešu tradīcijas un lokālo sarakstu kontekstā Kempe reprezentē vaboļu un kukaiņu aizbildni, kura ziņā ir nosargāt vaboļu skaitu un nodrošināti dabīgo līdzsvaru, simbolizējot rūpību un dabas sniegto velšu aizsardzību.

## Primary Source Details

- **Łasicki (Łas):** Records *Kempe* as a deity of the beetles (*pro scarabaeis*).
- **Vėlius (Vel):** Analyzed the functional role of the minor nature spirits in the context of the agricultural cycle and family safety.
- **VLE:** Mentions Kempe as one of the minor deities recorded in the 16th-century historiography.

## Key Metadata
- **Category**: Minor Nature Spirit / Genius Loci
- **Symbol**: Beetle, Hard shell, Flight (humming)
- **Function**: Protection and balance of the beetle population
- **Recorded in**: *De diis Samagitarum* (1582)
