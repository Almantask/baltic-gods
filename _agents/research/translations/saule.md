# Saulė / Saule

## Comprehensive Overview

Saulė (Lithuanian) and Saule (Latvian) is the Sun goddess, one of the most powerful and beloved deities in Baltic mythology. She is the mother of life, the protector of the unfortunate, and the source of warmth and fertility.

### English (EN)
**Saulė** is the life-giving goddess who traverses the sky in a golden chariot or a silver boat. She is often depicted as a radiant, golden-haired queen who weaves the light of the world. Saulė is the patron of orphans and a maternal figure who cares for the vulnerable. Her daily journey involves rising over the celestial mountain and descending into the sea at night to wash her horses. Her marriage to **Mėnulis** is the subject of many myths, often characterized by conflict and the Moon's unfaithfulness, which leads to **Perkūnas**'s intervention.

### Lithuanian (LT)
**Saulė** yra gyvybę teikianti deivė, šviesos ir šilumos motina, viena svarbiausių lietuvių mitologijos figūrų. Ji vaizduojama kaip spindinti valdovė, kasdien keliaujanti per dangų auksiniu vežimu, kurį traukia nepailstantys žirgai. Saulė yra ypač gera nuskriaustiesiems ir našlaičiams. Mitologijoje ji dažnai minima kaip **Mėnulio** žmona; jų santykiai yra audringi dėl Mėnulio neištikimybės Aušrinei, už ką jį baudžia Perkūnas. Saulė taip pat siejama su likimo audimu ir derliumi.

### Latvian (LV)
**Saule** latviešu mitoloģijā un dainās ir viena no centrālajām tēliem — „Saules māte”, kura dāvā dzīvību, gaismu un siltumu. Viņa jāj sudraba zirgos vai brauc zelta ratos pāri debesu kalnam. Saule ir bāreņu aizstāve un sargātāja. Dainās viņa bieži parādās kā čakla saimniece, kura vakarā dodas dzelmes ūdeņos mazgāt savus zirgus vai atpūsties. Saules un **Mēness** attiecības ir sarežģītas un bieži vien traģiskas, kas skaidro debess parādību ciklus un mītus par debesu kāzām.

## Primary Source Details

- **Łasicki (Łas):** Mentions the sun as a major object of veneration among the Samogitians, though with limited theological depth.
- **Vėlius (Vel):** Analyzes Saulė as a core symbol of the celestial tier, focusing on her maternal protection and the etiological myths explaining solar phenomena.
- **Barons (Bar):** Cataloged thousands of *dainas* dedicated to Saulė, where she is often addressed as "Māmuliņa" (Dear Mother), highlighting her deep emotional and spiritual connection to the Latvian people.
- **Biezais (Biez):** Provided the definitive study of the "Heavenly Family," detailing the complex relationships between the Sun and her daughters (Saules meitas).
- **VLE:** Synthesizes historical and linguistic research, confirming Saulė's role as the pre-eminent female deity of the bright day.
