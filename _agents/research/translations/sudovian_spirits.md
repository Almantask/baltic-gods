# Sudovian Book Minor Spirits / Pseudoteonimai

## Comprehensive Overview

The "Sudovian Book" Spirits include several deities—**Ischneputtins**, **Eraiczio**, **Goniglis**, and **Pocclusium**—whose authenticity as primary Prussian gods is highly debated. Most current scholarship classifies them as "pseudoteonimai," created by the author of the *Sudauer Büchlein* (16th c.) to map Baltic folk customs into a structured classical or Christian framework.

### Ischneputtins (Air and Breath)
- **Description**: Listed as the "God of Air and Breath." This being governs the atmospheric realm, winds, and the breath of life. It was to be appeased for favorable weather and health.
- **Academic Note**: Many scholars see this as a personification of the sky itself or a misinterpretation of an epithet.

### Eraiczio (Lamb and Spring)
- **Description**: The "God of Lambs." A specialized spring-rejuvenation spirit associated with the birth of livestock. Represented the fertility of the herds as the agricultural year began.
- **Academic Note**: The name is clearly derived from the Lithuanian word for "lamb" (*ėriukas*), suggesting a personification of the animal rather than a separate deity.

### Goniglis (Pasture/Herdsman)
- **Description**: The "God of Pastures." A protector of the cattle while they are grazing in the fields. Guardian against predators and ensures rich grass.
- **Academic Note**: Directly related to the Lithuanian **Ganiklis** (to graze/pasture). While a valid folk-belief figure, its elevation to a "god" in the triad-subordinate list is considered a late scholastic invention.

### Pocclusium (Infernal Spirit/Flying Demon)
- **Description**: The "Flying Demon of the Air" or "Underworld Messenger." While the deity **Peckols** represents the Lord of the Dead, **Pocclusium** is the active, spectral manifestation of the dead that "flies" through the world to haunt or warn the living.
- **Academic Note**: This is likely a duplication of **Peckols** (Pikulis) created by the chronicler to fit a more complex demonological model (separating the "King of Hades" from the "spirits of the air").

## Primary Source Details

- **Sudovian Book (Sud):** The primary and often sole source for these specific names. [Sudauer Büchlein, 16th c.]
- **Vėlius (Vel):** Critically analyzes the "Sudovian Book" list, noting the likely invention of many minor teonims. [Norbertas Vėlius, 1996]
- **Būga (Būga):** Connects several of the names to common nouns (lamb, pasture) rather than divine roots. [Kazimieras Būga, 1958]
- **VLE:** Describes these figures under the category of Prussian folk-belief and chronicler-distortions.

## Key Metadata
- **Region**: Old Prussia (Sudovia/Sūduva)
- **Tribe**: Sudovian / Old Prussian
- **Period**: 16th-century records (Late/Scholastic)
- **Category**: Minor Spirit / Folk Custom Personification / Pseudoteonimas
- **Symbol**: Breath/Air (Ischneputtins), Lamb (Eraiczio), Shepherd's Crozier (Goniglis), Ghostly shroud (Pocclusium)
- **Function**: Protecting specific trades/animals, Personifying atmospheric phenomena, Elaborating the underworld hierarchy
