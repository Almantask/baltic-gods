# Lake Moon Ladder / Ežero Mėnulio Kopėčios / Ezera Mēness Kāpnes

## Comprehensive Overview

**Lake Moon Ladder** is a creative/atmospheric sacred site set in the Aukštaitija lake district, Lithuania, inspired by well-attested Baltic beliefs about Mėnulis (the moon god) and his role in fishermen's practice. The lake name evokes the optical phenomenon of a long, narrow moon-reflection on calm water. The general connection between Mėnulis, fishermen's timing, and moonlit paths for ancestral souls is documented in Baltic folklore; this specific named site is a project-invented location not found in the scholarly record.

### English (EN)
**Mėnulis** was far more active and present than a modern conception of the moon would suggest: he rode a horse or drove a chariot across the night sky, he visited Saulė (the sun) as a husband who was periodically punished by her or by Perkūnas for his inconstancies, and he regulated the lives of fishermen by controlling the tides, the visibility of underwater life, and the behaviour of schools of fish. The "ladder" image of moonlight on still water was associated in Baltic tradition with ancestors ascending by night — the Latvian *dainas* have a well-developed motif of souls travelling by moonlight.

### Lithuanian (LT)
**Mėnulis** buvo daug aktyvesnis ir labiau esantis, nei šiuolaikinė mėnulio samprata reikštų: jis jojo arkliu ar vairavo karieta per nakties dangų, lankė Saulę kaip vyras, kurį ji ar Perkūnas periodiškai baudė dėl jo nepastovumų, ir reguliavo žvejų gyvenimą valdydamas potvynius, povandeninio gyvenimo matomumą ir žuvų mokyklų elgesį. Sidabrinės šviesos kopėčių įvaizdis ant ramo vandens baltiškojoje tradicijoje buvo siejamas su protėviais, kylančiais nakties keliu — latvių dainose gerai išplėtotas sielų kelionės mėnesienos motyvais.

### Latvian (LV)
**Mēness** bija daudz aktīvāks un klātesošāks, nekā mūsdienu mēness koncepcija norādītu: viņš jāja ar zirgu vai brauca ar karieti pāri nakts debesīm, apmeklēja Sauli kā vīrs, kuru viņa vai Pērkons periodiski sodīja par viņa nepastāvību, un regulēja zvejnieku dzīves, kontrolējot paisumus, zemūdens dzīves redzamību un zivju baru uzvedību. Sudraba gaismas kāpņu tēls uz klusa ūdens baltu tradīcijā tika saistīts ar senčiem, kas ceļo naktī — latviešu dainās ir labi attīstīts dvēseļu ceļošanas pa mēnessgaismu motīvs.

## Primary Source Details

- **Łasicki (Łas):** Notes *Mėnulis* as a Lithuanian deity counted among the sky family and consulted at night journeys and fishing. [Jan Łasicki, *De Diis Samagitarum*, 1615]
- **Barons (Bar):** Latvian *dainas* with extensive moon-traveler motifs, including the "ladder of moonlight" image and souls ascending by night. [Krišjānis Barons, *Latvju dainas*, 6 vols, 1894–1915]
- **Biezais (Biez):** Studies the moon-god's role in the Latvian sky family; his relationship with sun, pleiades, and the fate of fishermen. [Haralds Biezais, *Die himmlische Götterfamilie*, 1972]
- **VLE:** Encyclopaedia entry on *Mėnulis* and his role in Lithuanian night-travel and fisher's belief (*vle.lt*)
- **Šmits (Šmits):** Folk accounts from Latvian lake regions linking moonlight reflections to deceased souls and fishing luck. [Pēteris Šmits, *Latviešu pasakas un teikas*, Vol. 9, 1930]
