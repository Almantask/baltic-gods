# Lazdona / Lasdona

## Comprehensive Overview

"Lazdona" (Lithuanian) and "Lasdona" (Latvian) are specific nature deities associated with the hazel tree (*lazdynas*) and its nuts. In Baltic mythology, the hazel was considered a sacred tree with protective qualities, and its nut harvest was a vital food source.

### English (EN)
**Lazdona** is the "Goddess of Hazel Trees" or the "Goddess of Nuts." She oversees the hazel groves, which were prized by the Baltic tribes because their nuts provided essential nourishment and were used in rituals and divinations. The hazel is traditionally believed to have protective properties against lightning and snakes, as it is a sacred tree of the forest undergrowth. Lazdona ensures that the bushes are bountiful and that their fruits ripen correctly. In folklore, her name is often linked to the **Lazduona** river, suggesting a connection between water and the lush vegetation of the groves. She is a specialized nature deity, representing the specific abundance of the forest's fruit-bearing plants.

### Lithuanian (LT)
**Lazdona** lietuvių mitologijoje yra lazdynų ir riešutų deivė. Jos vardas pirmą kartą paminėtas Jono Lasickio (*Jan Łasicki*) 1615 metais kaip *Lasdona*. Lazdynas senovės lietuvių pasaulėžiūroje buvo šventas medis, tikėta, kad jis saugo nuo žaibo (nes Perkūnas jo „nemuša“) ir gyvačių. Deivė Lazdona globonanti riešutų derlių, o jos vardas taip pat siejamas su upėvardžiu Lazduona. Jos kultas rodo archajišką gamtos personifikaciją, kai kiekvienam svarbiam augalui ar giraitės daliai būdavo priskiriama atskira sėkmę nešanti dvasia ar dievybė.

### Latvian (LV)
**Lazdu māte** latviešu mitoloģijā atbilst lietuviešu Lazdonai. Viņa ir lazdu krūmu un riekstu māte, kas sargā šo vērtīgo meža velti. Dainās lazda tiek minēta kā ziedu koks vai pat kā koks ar dzelzs saknēm, kas sargā pret ļaunumu. Lazdu māte rudenī dāvā riekstus saimei, un mednieki vai meža gājēji uzrunā viņu ar cieņu. Tiek uzskatīts, ka lasdu maiga daba un riekstu cietais apvalks simbolizē dabas spēka gudrību un izturību. Viņas loma ir specifiska rudenim un meža veltēm.

## Primary Source Details

- **Łasicki (Łas):** Records *Lasdona* as the goddess of nuts (*cerasos tuebatur* - though sometimes misidentified as cherry god Kirnis, Lazdona specifically refers to hazelnuts). [Jan Łasicki, 1615]
- **Vėlius (Vel):** Notes the mythological significance of the hazel tree as a protected plant in Baltic folk belief. [Norbertas Vėlius]
- **VLE:** Describes Lazdona as the personified spirit of the hazel grove.
- **Barons (Bar):** Cataloged Latvian *dainas* dedicated to Lazdu māte and the ritual significance of hazelnuts.

## Key Metadata
- **Region**: Lithuania, Samogitia
- **Tribe**: Lithuanian, Latvian
- **Period**: Medieval to 17th-century folklore
- **Category**: Nature Spirit / Fruit and Nut Deity
- **Symbol**: Hazelnut, Hazel branch, Hazel staff
- **Animals**: Squirrel (often associated with nut harvest)
- **Function**: Protection of hazel groves, Nutrient-rich nut harvest, Protection against lightning and snakes
- **Etymology**: *lazd- (hazel/rod)
