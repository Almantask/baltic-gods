# Srutis / Algis / Prigirstis / Šlaitis

## Comprehensive Overview

Srutis, Algis, Prigirstis, and Šlaitis represent some of the many specialized minor Lithuanian (Samogitian) deities and spirits recorded in the 16th-century list of Jan Łasicki. They are guardians of specific natural features and the spiritual communication.

### English (EN)
**Srutis**, **Algis**, **Prigirstis**, and **Šlaitis** are the "Minor Voices" of the household and its environment. Srutis (historically recorded as *Srutim*) is the deity of the flowing river current, distinct from the generalized river spirit **Upinis**. Algis (historically as *Algi*) is a specialized deity often associated with messaging or as an angelic intermediary between gods and humans. Prigirstis (meaning "The Listener") is a deity believed to hear even the softest whispered prayers. Šlaitis (meaning "Hillside Spirit") is the guardian of the sloping terrain and natural features of the farmstead. These figures represents the "micro-pantheon" where every activity and feature was believed to have its own divine overseer.

### Lithuanian (LT)
**Srutis**, **Algis**, **Prigirstis** ir **Šlaitis** lietuvių mitologijoje yra deivės/dvasios, atsakingos už konkrečias gamtos ir dvasinio ryšio sritis. Algis (Lasickio sąraše – *Algi*) yra dangiškasis pasiuntinys, sargiantis ryšį tarp dievų ir žmonių. Prigirstis (nuo „prigirsti“ – išgirsti) nulemia sklandų maldų išklausymą, o Srutis ir Šlaitis atitinkamai globoja upės srovę bei kalvos šlaitą. Jų buvimas Lasickio sąraše rodo, kad senovės lietuviai siekė sakralizuoti savo aplinką, tikėdami, kad kiekvienai gyvenimo akimirkai reikia dieviškos pagalbos.

### Latvian (LV)
**Srutis**, **Algis**, **Prigirstis** un **Šlaitis** latviešu mitoloģijā nav tikuši atsevišķi izdalīti, tā vietā to funkcijas veica dažādas **Zāļu mātes** vai **Saules meitų** dāvātā prieka ir svētības izpausmes. Tomēr lietuviešu tradīcijas un lokālo sarakstu kontekstā šie tēli reprezentē sētas ir istabu aizbildņus, kura ziņā ir nosargāt sakrāto labību ir nodrošinti ziemas pārtikas krājumus, simbolizējot rūpību ir dabas sniegto velšu aizsardzību.

## Primary Source Details

- **Łasicki (Łas):** Records *Srutim* (river current), *Algi* (angel/messenger), *Prigirszczium* (listener), and *Szlaitym* (hillside) among the Samogitian gods.
- **Vėlius (Vel):** Analyzed the functional role of the minor spirits in the context of the natural and social cycle.
- **VLE:** Mentions these figures as one of the minor deities recorded in the 16th-century historiography.

## Key Metadata
- **Category**: Household / Nature / Social Spirit
- **Symbol**: River ripple, Ear, Wing, Hillside slope, Message scroll
- **Function**: Protection of natural features and communication with the divine
- **Recorded in**: *De diis Samagitarum* (1582)
