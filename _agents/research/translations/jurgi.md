# Jurģi — Latvian Spring Cattle-Driving Festival

## Comprehensive Overview

**Jurģi** (April 23, St. George's Day) is the Latvian spring festival marking the official first driving of cattle onto spring pasture, the beginning of the herding and dairy season, and the activation of outdoor agricultural life. While the Catholic St. George lends his name, the ritual content overlaps substantially with the parallel **Ūsiņdiena** (horse festival) and with pre-Christian cattle-protection rites. Together they frame the full spring opening of outdoor animal husbandry.

### English (EN)
**Jurģi** (April 23) is the Latvian spring cattle-driving day — the official beginning of the outdoor herding season. In the morning, cattle are driven out of their winter stalls for the first time using freshly cut rowan or birch branches (*pātagi*), believed to purify the animals and protect them from evil spirits and diseases for the entire summer. The threshold of the stable is swept the night before, and protective symbols (crosses or solar marks) are chalked on the stable doorposts. The first herb collected on Jurģi morning — usually the first green grass or a sprig of rowan — is fed to the cattle to build their resistance to disease and ensure good milk production. The cattle herd is driven three times around a bonfire (*ugunskurs*) or through a smoke-fumigation to further purify them. **Lauku māte** (Mother of Fields), **Lopu māte** (Mother of Livestock), and **Piena māte** (Mother of Milk) are the primary supernatural patrons of Jurģi — each is invoked with short spoken or sung formulae. Jurģi is also the traditional day for beginning annual farm-service contracts and for farmhands to move to new employers.

### Lithuanian (LT)
**Jurģi** (latv., liet. *Jurgines* / *Šv. Jurgio diena*) — latvių balandžio 23 d. šventė, pirmojo galvijų išganymo simbolis. Galvijai pirmą kartą išvarinėjami iš tvartų su šermukšnio ar beržo šakelėmis, valyti nuo ligų. Slenksčiai pakabintais apsaugu simboliais. Pirmoji žolė ar šermukšnio šakelė atiduodama galvijams. Galvijai tris kartus apsukami aplink laužą ar rūkymą. Kviečiami **Lauku māte**, **Lopu māte**, **Piena māte**.

### Latvian (LV)
**Jurģi** ir galvenais lopkopja pavasara svētks — pirmā lopu izvešana ganībās pēc ziemas. Pātagas jeb pītās rīkstītes no pīlādža vai bērza aizsargā dzīvniekus no slimībām un ļauniem gariem. Kūts slieksnis tiek iezīmēts ar aizsargzīmēm. Pirmā pavasara zāle un pīlādža zariņi tiek dotas dzīvniekiem, lai stiprinātu to veselību. Lopu māte, lauku māte un piena māte tiek godātas ar dziesmu formulām. Jurģi ir arī kalpošanas kontraktu beigu un sākuma diena — kalpiem iespēja mainīt saimnieku, bet saimniekiem — nolīgt jaunus. Šajā dienā sākas pilna vasaras lauksaimnieciskā aktivitāte.

## Primary Source Details

- **Barons (Bar):** Spring *dainas* for Jurģi, including livestock-protection formulae and Lauku māte invocations. [Krišjānis Barons, *Latvju dainas*, 1894–1915]
- **LNE:** Encyclopaedic entry on Jurģi traditions, cattle-driving customs, and farmhand-contract practices. [*Latvijas Nacionālā enciklopēdija*]
- **Šmits (Šmits):** Documents the purification-with-rowan, smoke-fumigation, and doorpost-marking customs. [Pēteris Šmits, *Latviešu mitoloģija*, 1926]

## Key Metadata
- **Region**: Latvia-wide
- **Tribe**: Latvians
- **Period**: Pre-Christian cattle-protection rite; aligned with Christian St. George (April 23) from medieval period
- **Category**: Calendar Festival / Animal-Protection / Spring-Agricultural Ritual
- **Date**: April 23 (St. George's Day)
- **Symbol**: Rowan branch (*pātaga*), Bonfire, Smoke fumigation, Doorpost cross-marking, First spring grass
- **Deities invoked**: Lauku māte (field mother), Lopu māte (livestock mother), Piena māte (milk mother), Ūsiņš (complementary horse deity)
- **Ritual actions**: Cattle-driving with rowan branches, bonfire-circling, smoke-fumigation, first-grass feeding, doorpost protection marking
