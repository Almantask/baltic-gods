# Baltic Mythology Research Sources

To find authentic tales/legends with specific metadata (region, tribe, year, references), the researcher agent should consult these primary and secondary sources:

## Digital Archives
- **Lithuanian Mythology (LLTI)**: [llti.lt](http://www.llti.lt/) - Institute of Lithuanian Literature and Folklore.
- **Latvian Folklore Archives (LFK)**: [lfk.lv](http://lfk.lv/en) - Archives of Latvian Folklore.
- **Dainu Skapis**: [dainuskapis.lv](http://www.dainuskapis.lv/) - Digital collection of Latvian folksongs.
- **Myth.lt**: [myth.lt](http://myth.lt/en/) - Specialized portal for Lithuanian mythology and folklore.

## Encyclopedias & Academic Sources
- **Britannica**: [Baltic Religion](https://www.britannica.com/topic/Baltic-religion)
- **World History Encyclopedia**: [Baltic Mythology](https://www.worldhistory.org/category/baltic-mythology/)
- **Norbertas Vėlius**: Principal researcher of Lithuanian mythology. Seek his works like "The World Outlook of the Ancient Lithuanians".
- **Gintaras Beresnevičius**: Essays on Baltic religion and afterlife.

## Key Metadata Markers to Seek
- **Region**: Look for "Aukštaitija", "Žemaitija" (Samogitia), "Dzūkija", "Suvalkija", "Kurzeme", "Latgale", "Vidzeme".
- **Tribe**: "Latvians", "Lithuanians", "Old Prussians", "Curonians", "Semigallians", "Jotvingians".
- **Year/Period**: Note early mentions (e.g., 14th century chroniclers like Peter von Dusburg, or 16th century records like Jan Łasicki).
- **References**: Always cite the collector (e.g., Jonas Basanavičius, Krišjānis Barons) or the archive ID.
