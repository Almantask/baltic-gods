The Krustpils castle mound, overlooking the Daugava river, once served as a strategic and ritual intersection where the lands of the Selonian and Latgalian peoples met. This gateway was blessed for protection and commerce by the gods.

Krustpils continues to be a site of great spiritual and political resonance for eastern Latvia. Its position marks the boundary where the tribal regions overlap, creating a fertile space for communication between cultures and the divine.
