Twangste was the primary fort of the Sambians on the Pregel river. The name itself suggests a place near a dam or a quiet water, a contrast to the fierce military importance the site would hold during the 13th-century uprisings.

Built on a ridge, Twangste was a center for the Sambian nobility, a place of council and ritual. Its destruction and replacement by the Teutonic castle of Königsberg in 1255 symbolized the decapitation of the Prussian social order, yet the site remains the geographic heart of the region's ancient history.
