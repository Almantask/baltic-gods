The Dubingiai Lake Sanctuary is a rare example of a high-status pagan site that transitioned into the era of the Grand Duchy's nobility. Located near Lake Asveja, archaeologists discovered pits containing the remains of animal sacrifices, suggesting a significant ritual center.

The sanctuary was likely dedicated to Perkūnas, the god of justice and lightning, and served as a place where the ruling elite would validate their power through ancient rites. Its location by the deep, clear waters of the lake added a layer of purification and spiritual depth to the ceremonies performed here.
