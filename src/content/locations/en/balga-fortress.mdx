Balga, originally the Prussian fort of Honeida, sits on a peninsula overlooking the Vistula Lagoon. Its capture by the Teutonic Order in 1237 marked a turning point in the Prussian Crusade, as it gave the crusaders a permanent naval base to strike deep into the heart of the coastal tribes.

The transition from a wooden Prussian stronghold to a stone Teutonic castle represents the violent shift in regional power. However, the site's strategic importance and its proximity to the waters of Autrimpas remained, a silent witness to the end of the Pomesanian and Warmian sovereignty.
