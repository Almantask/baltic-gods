The Mežotne mound was the administrative and ritual heart of the Semigallian people. Rising above the Lielupe river, it was a fortified sanctuary where the tribal elders and priests gathered to make decisions and honor the earth-mother, Māra.

As a significant regional center, Mežotne guarded the fertile plains of Zemgale. Its spiritual importance was tied to the prosperity of the land, and it was here that the first-fruits of the harvest were offered to ensure the community's survival through the harsh northern winters.
