Tērvete was the seat of the legendary Semigallian King Nameisis, a figure of resistance and sovereign power. The hill itself was regarded in myth as a 'living entity', a guardian that felt the footsteps of its people and the steel of its enemies.

The castle mound remains a site of national pride, representing the era when the Baltic kings ruled their own lands. Its aura is one of strength and earth-rooted wisdom, where the spirits of the ancient warriors are still said to watch over the pine forests of Zemgale.
