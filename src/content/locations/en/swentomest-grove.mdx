Swentomest, later known as Heiligenbeil, was a 'Holy Town' of the Natangians. Its life was centered around a sacred grove and an ancient oak tree that were considered the most holy objects in the region. The town's name itself is a testament to its ritual importance.

When the Teutonic Order destroyed the grove, they intended to sever the Natangians' connection to their gods. Yet, the memory of the 'Holy Place' persisted in the name of the town for centuries, a reminder of the thousands who once gathered under the oak's canopy to offer their prayers to Žemyna.
