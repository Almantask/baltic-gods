The Hill of Crosses is a unique monument to the persistence of the human spirit. While its surface is covered in Catholic symbols, its roots lie in an ancient pagan hillfort. It represents a layered history where the old sacredness of the mound was never forgotten, only transformed.

Quietly walking between the thousands of wooden, metal, and stone crosses, one can hear the wind whistling through them—a sound that locals once interpreted as the voices of the mound's original guardians. It remains a site of profound silence and collective memory.
