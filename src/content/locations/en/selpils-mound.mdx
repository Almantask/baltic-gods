Sēlpils was the capital of the Selonians, a Baltic tribe known for their fierce independence and unique cultural identity. This hillfort on the Daugava was not only a military stronghold but also a sacred repository for Selonian traditions and rituals.

The site reflects the strategic and spiritual importance of the Daugava as a borderland. It was here that the Selonians maintained their connection to the high gods, even as the crusading orders encroached upon their ancestral territories.
