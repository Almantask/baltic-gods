Medvėgalis is the highest peak in Samogitia and was once its most formidable fortress. During the long wars against the Teutonic Order, it served as both a defensive stronghold and a spiritual refuge, where the old gods were invoked to protect the land.

The complex of seven hills was seen as a sacred geometry, a place where the earthly and divine planes were closely aligned. From its summit, the entire Samogitian landscape unfolds, a reminder of the vast territory once protected by the spirits of the warriors and priestesses who called this hill their home.
