The elusive Beverīna, a legendary Latgalian castle mentioned in the *Chronicle of Henry of Livonia*, remains a site of mystery and divine musical protection. Stories tell of sacred singers who averted a siege through the power of sacred song.

Though its exact geographic location remains debated, Beverīna exists as a symbol of spiritual defiance and Latgalian culture, a place where the gods intervened not with iron, but with the resonance of the word and the melody.
