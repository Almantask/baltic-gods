Girgždūtė is one of the highest hills in Samogitia, featuring a distinctive dual-ridge structure. Legend and archaeology suggest that while one peak was reserved for defense, the other served as a profound pagan shrine where ritual fires burned for centuries.

The longevity of the cult activities at Girgždūtė indicates its primary importance as a ritual center. It was a site prioritized for lightning-rituals and fire-offerings, where the Samogitian people sought the direct intervention of Perkūnas to protect their autonomy and their way of life.
