The confluence of the Nemunas and Neris rivers in Kaunas is a place of massive elemental power. In the pre-Christian era, it was believed that the meeting of these two great waters was a sacred event, overseen by Perkūnas himself.

During the spring thaws, the crashing of ice at the confluence was seen as a divine battle, clearing the way for the new year. Rituals involving fire and water were frequently held at the tip of the land to ensure the gods' favor for the coming harvest and the safety of the river-borne trade.
