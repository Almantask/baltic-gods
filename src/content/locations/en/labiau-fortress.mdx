Labiau, or Labguva, was a strategic Skalvian fort guarding the vital waterways between the Deime river and the Curonian Lagoon. It was a place where the life of the people was inextricably linked to the movement of the tides and the favor of the water-god, Patrimpas.

As a nodal point for both trade and defense, Labguva was a site of frequent ritual. Offerings were thrown into the waters to ensure safe passage and successful fishing, reflecting a worldview where every journey required a spiritual acknowledgment of the deep.
