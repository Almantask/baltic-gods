The site of the Battle of Durbė (1260) represents the greatest pagan victory over the combined forces of the Teutonic and Livonian Orders. It was here that the Samogitians and Curonians, invoking the gods of their land, decisively crushed the crusader iron.

Durbė is a sacred battlefield, a place where the intervention of Perkūnas was seen in the sudden turn of the tide. The victory sparked a massive, Baltic-wide uprising that preserved the indigenous traditions and territorial sovereignty for generations, making it a site of eternal resonance for the Baltic spirit.
