# Rumšiškės: The Living Hearth of Baltic Folklore

The **Lithuanian Open-Air Museum** at **Rumšiškės** is much more than a museum. It is a living, breathing landscape of reconstructed villages from all four ethnographic regions of Lithuania: **Aukštaitija**, **Dzūkija**, **Suvalkija**, and **Žemaitija**.

## The Spirit of the Home-Fire

Central to the Rumšiškės experience is the **hearth**, or **krosnis**. In every reconstructed farmhouse, the focus is on the stove and its tutelary goddess, **Gabija**.
- **Domestic Rites**: The museum demonstrates daily rituals involving fire: "covering" the embers for the night and "waking" them in the morning with a prayer.
- **Calendar Festivals**: Rumšiškės is the primary site for the **Užgavėnės** (winter-to-spring carnival) and **Joninės** (Midsummer) celebrations, where the entire nation gathers to perform ancient dances and ritual burnings (Morė).

## Architectural Sanctuary

Each of the museum's buildings is an authentic original, painstakingly moved and reassembled. This makes Rumšiškės a "sanctuary" for the architectural and spiritual **genii loci** (localized spirits) of a lifestyle that has largely vanished from the modern countryside.

## Deep Legacy

- **Sacred Stones**: Replicas of various legendary stones (like the **Velnias** stones) are scattered in the wooded areas.
- **Folklore Preservation**: The museum's researchers and staff are the leading practitioners of Baltic folk music and dance.
- **The Kauno marios Connection**: Rumšiškės was established on the banks of the reservoir, a new home for the spirits of the villages that were flooded in the valley below.
