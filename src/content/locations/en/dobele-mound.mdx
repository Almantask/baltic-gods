Dobele was one of the most formidable Semigallian strongholds, a place that famously resisted six separate sieges by the Livonian Order. Its eventual fall in 1289 did not signify defeat, but rather the Semigallians' resolve to burn their own sanctuary rather than see it desecrated.

The mound is a site of profound historical memory, representing the 'scorched earth' sacrifice of a people who chose exile and rebirth over submission. Its aura of strength and maternal protection, associated with Māra, continues to resonate through the plains of Zemgale.
