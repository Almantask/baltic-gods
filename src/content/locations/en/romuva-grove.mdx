# Romuva Sacred Grove: The Eternal Flame

**Romuva** (or **Romowe**) stands as the arguably most significant, yet most elusive, spiritual center of the entire Baltic world. Located in the ancient **Nadruvia** region of **Old Prussia**, it was first described in detail by Peter of Dusburg and later expanded on by Simon Grunau.

## The Central Sanctuary

At its heart stood a massive, evergreen **sacred oak** dedicated to the three supreme Prussian gods:
1.  **Perkūnas**: The Sky God of Thunder and Order.
2.  **Patrimpas**: The Green God of Spring and Prosperity.
3.  **Patollo**: The White God of the Dead and Secrets.

Legend speaks of the **eternal flame** (Ugnis) that was guarded here day and night by the **Vaidilutės** (vestal virgins) and supervised by the **Kriwe-Kriwaitis**, the high priest with spiritual authority over all Baltic tribes.

## The Nexus of Laws

Romuva served not only as a place of sacrifice but also as a supreme court where:
- Tribal disputes were settled.
- Marriages and major alliances were consecrated.
- The laws of gods and men were interpreted for the entire Prussian-Lithuanian-Latvian world.

While its exact location remains a matter of archaeological debate, its symbolic "aura" is the foundation of the modern **Romuva** reconstructionist movement in Lithuania.
