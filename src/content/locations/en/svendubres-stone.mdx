Švendubrė's stone in the Raigardas valley is one of the clearest cases where a Lithuanian mythological boulder can be described from both folklore and archaeology at once. VLE identifies it explicitly as **Švendubrės akmuo**, also called **Velnio akmuo**, and notes the cup-marks on its eastern side, a feature that links it to the wider group of Baltic ritual stones dated to the Bronze and Early Iron Age.

The local legends are thoroughly chthonic. Velnias is said to have carried the stone in order to dam the Nemunas, but the crowing of a rooster forced him to drop it before the river could be blocked. Later memory layered Christian and modern history onto the same boulder: villagers protected it from being broken for church construction, a cross was fixed into it in 1936, and twentieth-century researchers from Tarasenka to Vaitkevičius treated it as both a myth-bearing monument and an archaeological object.
