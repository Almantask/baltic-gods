The Panemunė hill fort on the banks of the Nemunas river was a key outpost in the early defense of the Grand Duchy against the Teutonic Order. Its strategic location allowed the Baltic tribes to control one of the region's most important waterways.

The mound was more than a fortification; it acted as a ritual point where the light of signal fires served as the eye of Perkūnas, watching over the northern frontier and warning the heartlands of approaching storm.
