Romuva, also known as Rickoyoto, was the legendary spiritual capital of the Baltic world. Located in the heart of Nadruvia, it was the seat of the Kriwe-Kriwajto, a high priest whose authority was respected by tribes from the Vistula to the Daugava.

The center of the sanctuary was a massive evergreen oak tree, partitioned into three sections to house the images of the primary trinity: Perkūnas, Patrimpas, and Patollos. The eternal fire of Romuva served as the heartbeat of the Prussian resistance, a symbol of a unified spiritual identity that transcended tribal borders.
