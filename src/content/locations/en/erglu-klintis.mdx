Ērgļu Klintis, or the Eagle Cliffs, rise vertically from the Gauja river. Anciently known as 'Pieškalnu klintis' (God's cliffs), they were believed to be a place where the majestic eagles of the sky-gods rested between their journeys.

The sheer scale of the sandstone walls creates a natural cathedral-like atmosphere. It was a site prioritized for sky-rituals, where offerings were made to ensure the favor of Perkūnas and the protection of the valley's inhabitants.
