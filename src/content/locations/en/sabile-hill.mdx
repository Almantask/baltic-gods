The Sabile wine hill, while famous for its unique northern vineyard, sits on a historic site that has served as a ritual landscape in the Abava valley since the medieval era. It was here that the Curonian tribes gathered for agricultural rites and seasonal festivals.

The hill represents the ancient Baltic tradition of sacred agriculture, reflecting the blessings of the earth mother and the growth of the vine—symbols of both human ingenuity and the landscape's ability to provide abundance even in high latitudes.
