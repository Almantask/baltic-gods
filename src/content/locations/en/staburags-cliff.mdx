Staburags was once a magnificent limestone cliff on the Daugava river that 'wept' water from its springs. In folklore, the cliff was a girl who turned to stone from grief, and its tears were the source of the river's power. It was the legendary home of the goddess Staburadze.

Though submerged in 1965 by the construction of a hydroelectric plant, Staburags remains a potent symbol of Latvian cultural loss and endurance. It lives on in poems, songs, and the national epic Lāčplēsis, representing the deep emotional connection between the people and their land.
