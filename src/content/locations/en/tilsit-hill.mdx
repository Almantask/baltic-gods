Tilsit Hill rises above the expansive Nemunas valley, serving as a ceremonial mirror to the even more famous Rambynas across the river. For the Skalvian tribe, these two heights formed a sacred gateway, a landscape of fire and water.

While Rambynas was the site of communal festivals, Tilsit was a place where individual clans and warriors sought the favor of Perkūnas. The high ground offered a wide view of the river, connecting the people to the celestial cycles and the seasonal rhythms of the Baltic heartland.
