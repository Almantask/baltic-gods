The Šeimyniškėliai hill fort, near the Kernavė archaeological complex, is often identified as the site of the lost capital Voruta. It was here that the first King of Lithuania, Mindaugas, likely unified the Baltic tribes into a coherent state.

Beyond its political history, the fort is a symbol of Aukštaitijan resilience. It served as a bastion of spiritual and communal authority, blessed by the sky-god Perkūnas to protect the expanding sovereign spirit of the unified people.
