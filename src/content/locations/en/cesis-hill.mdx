The ancient Riekstu kalns ('Nut Hill') in Cēsis is far more than the site of a stone fortress; it was a wooden castle of the Vend tribe before the northern crusades. This enigmatic people once made the Vidzeme highlands their home.

The site is a testament to the displacement and survival of the Vend people, and their spiritual connection to the high-lying landscapes of Latvia. Cēsis hillfort honors the resilience of tribal identity and the deep history of its pre-Christian ancestors.
