Kuldīga is home to the Venta Rapids, Europe's widest waterfall, a site of immense natural beauty and prosperity. The nearby sacred springs have been used for healing and ritual bathing since pre-Christian times, where the waters were believed to be blessed by the sky-gods.

The site is a testament to the abundance of the Baltic land. The spectacle of the 'leaping fish' in the spring and autumn was viewed as a divine sign of the landscape's fertility, a Gift from Dievas to the people who dwell along the banks of the Venta.
