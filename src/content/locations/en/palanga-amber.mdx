The Palanga coast is more than just sand; it is the graveyard of a goddess's palace. After the North Wind rages, the sea yields golden shards of amber—the remains of Jūratė's underwater kingdom, shattered by Perkūnas's lightning.

Local folk gather these shards not as mere trinkets, but as tears of the sun. Each piece of amber is believed to hold a fragment of the warmth that Saulė gave to the original sea-palace, a reminder of a time when the sky and sea were closer.
