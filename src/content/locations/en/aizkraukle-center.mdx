The ancient Aizkraukle tribal center, a major stronghold of the Daugava Livs, once served as one of the most important trading and defensive hubs on the banks of the Daugava river between the Bronze Age and the 13th century.

It was more than a fortification; Aizkraukle was a ritual center blessed by the thunder-god Perkūnas, where tribal congresses and sun-watch ceremonies were held for generations before the arrival of the foreign stone fortresses.
