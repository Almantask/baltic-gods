The Salantai Burial Mounds are silent witnesses to the transition between life and death. Giltinė, the pale goddess with the long tongue, is said to walk these ridges during the heavy autumn mists.

Families visit these mounds not just to remember their ancestors, but to seek Giltinė's mercy for those currently ill. It is a place of heavy silence, where even the birds seem to fly lower, honoring the boundary between world and underworld.
