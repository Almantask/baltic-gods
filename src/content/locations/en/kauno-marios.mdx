# Kauno marios: The Sunken Villages and River Spirits

The **Kaunas Reservoir**, known locally as the **Kaunas Sea**, is Lithuania's largest artificial lake. Completed in 1959, it flooded a vast stretch of the **Nemunas** valley, submerging dozens of ancient villages, sacred springs, and ritual sites.

## A Modern Atlantis

While created for hydroelectric power, the flooding of the Nemunas valley gave rise to a "modern mythos." The people of the sunken villages—such as **Rumšiškės** (original site), **Dvareliškės**, and **Žemieji Amaliai**—carried their stories and folklore from the valley floor to the new highlands. Many believe the spirits of the drowned lands, the **Upiniai** and **Vandeniai**, still guard the underwater ruins.

## Mythic Legacy

- **The Nemunas Dragon**: Legends of serpentine river monsters found new life in the deep waters of the reservoir.
- **Lost Sacred Sites**: Several documented "pagan altars" and holy springs lie beneath the surface, occasionally revealed when water levels are low.
- **Modern Connection**: The site is a place of memory and reflection for those whose ancestral lands were claimed by the "marriage of fire (electricity) and water."
