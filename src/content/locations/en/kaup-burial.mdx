Kaup, located near Wiskiauten on the Sambian peninsula, was a site where the 'Amber Road' met the sea. Archaeological excavations have revealed a massive complex of burial mounds where Prussian and Scandinavian elites were laid to rest with unparalleled wealth.

The site is a testament to the sophistication of Sambian trade and the depth of their interaction with the wider Viking world. The burials reflect a culture that viewed the afterlife with great gravity, providing their dead with the tools and ornaments needed to navigate the realm of Patolla with honor.
