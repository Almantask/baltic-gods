The Valatkoniai Devil's Stone is a more narrowly attested site than Švendubrė's boulder, but it is still important because it shows how Lithuanian mythological geography can crystallize around a single erratic. VLE describes it as **Velnio akmuo** or **Valatkonių akmuo**, a protected split boulder in Baisogala forest whose geological scale and strange surface helped it become a legend-bearing landmark.

The folklore attached to it is compact and revealing. In one version, the devil was carrying the stone to destroy Baisogala church, but a rooster's crow forced him to drop it before dawn. In another, the boulder became entangled with a dispute over estate boundaries, so that infernal power entered the story through land, law, and movement rather than direct destruction. The result is a site whose sacred force lies less in documented ritual than in the persistence of place-bound legend.
