Lielvārde is synonymous with the destiny of the Latvian people. The park by the Daugava river is home to the 'Stone of Lāčplēsis', where the epic hero is said to have rested. It is also the spiritual origin of the Lielvārde belt, whose geometric patterns are believed to encode the secrets of the universe.

The site is a place where myth and craftsmanship intersect. It honors the goddess Māra and the human capacity to weave protection and meaning into the fabric of daily life, connecting the ancient symbols to the modern national identity.
