# Rundale Surroundings: The Silk and the Soil

While the famed **Rundāle Palace** is a baroque masterpiece of the 18th century, the fields and hillocks of the surrounding **Zemgale** countryside have a spiritual history that goes much deeper into the earth.

## The White Lady and the Ancestors

The local folklore of the **Zemgaļi** (Semigallians) speaks of the **Baltā dāma** (White Lady) whose apparition is seen in the park's alleyways. While often framed as a ghost story, her roots lie in the ancient cult of **Laima** (Fate) and the ancestral spirits that ensure the prosperity of the land.

## A Ritual Landscape

The area is dotted with:
- **Hill Forts**: Remnants of the Semigallian warriors who fiercely resisted the Livonian Order.
- **Sacred Stones**: Large boulders half-buried in the fertile soil, once used for seasonal offerings to the **Māte** spirits (Mothers) of fertility.
- **Underground Waters**: Mythical springs that supposedly feed the palace's canals, believed to be the dwellings of river sirens and water spirits.

## Modern Reflection

Rundāle represents the layered nature of the Baltic landscape, where the refined "silk" of the European Enlightenment was laid upon the foundational "soil" of persistent, earth-bound ancestry.
