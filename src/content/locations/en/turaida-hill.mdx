Turaida Hill, rising above the Gauja river valley, is a landscape where history and legend are inseparable. The site is famous for the medieval Turaida Castle, but its spiritual heart lies in the memory of the 'Rose of Turaida', a tale of devotion and tragic sacrifice.

The hill's significance is tied to the concept of moral purity and the protection of the land. It remains a place where the community honors the memory of those who stood firm in their values, reflecting the protective and maternal aura of the goddess Māra.
