Ragnit, or Ragainė, was a formidable hillfort of the Skalvian people on the Nemunas river. It served as a northern shield for the Prussian lands, a place where the community gathered for both defense and to celebrate the rites of Perkūnas.

The hillfort's significance was both military and religious. It was believed that as long as the altar fires of Ragainė burned, the Skalvian spirits would remain undefeated. Its eventual fall to the Order in 1289 was a heavy blow to the northern Baltic alliance, marking the advance of the cross into the deep wilderness.
