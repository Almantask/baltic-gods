The Žaltvykslė Spring is a gateway to the hidden world. Its name is tied to the 'žaltvykslės'—mysterious lights or will-o'-the-wisps that were said to guide or mislead travelers at night. These lights were seen as the manifestations of the Žaltys, the sacred grass snake.

Local lore warns that the spring is a portal to the underworld, where the grass-snake king holds court. Offerings of milk were once left at such springs to ensure the protection of the household and to keep the connection between the mortal plane and the spirit world healthy.
