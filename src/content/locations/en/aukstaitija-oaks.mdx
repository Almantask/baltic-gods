The split oaks here act like tuned bells after every lightning strike. Elders wedge iron nails into the seams, then listen for which direction the ringing echoes; that echo decides where herders will drive their cattle before the next storm line.

At solstice, children race between trunks carrying barley garlands. They hang the garlands high so the wind can steal a few grains—an offering meant to keep hail from flattening new shoots.***
