The mythological stones of Žagarė, located in the flatlands of northern Lithuania, are ancient markers of a petrified spirit world. These stones have been central to local cults for centuries, believed to be the home of earth spirits and guardians.

Žagarė's ritual landscape highlights the ancient Baltic tradition of stone-worship, where the density and endurance of granite were viewed as attributes of the earth goddess, Žemyna, anchoring the human community to the deep soil of their Samogitian ancestors.
