The Joniškis sacred grove is a remnant of the ancient ritual landscape that spanned the border between Samogitia and Zemgale. In this boundary zone, the people of the flatlands gathered for rituals that ensured the harmony of overlapping tribal territories.

This site was a space of mediation, where tribal congresses and religious ceremonies were held overseen by the high gods. For the people of northern Lithuania, such groves were the primary sites of celestial contact and seasonal ritual.
