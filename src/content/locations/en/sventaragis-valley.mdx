Šventaragis Valley, located at the heart of Vilnius where the rivers Neris and Vilnia meet, was the final resting place of the pagan Grand Dukes. It was Duke Šventaragis who first decreed that rulers be cremated here, ensuring their spirits joined the high gods through the sacred smoke.

Archaeologists have found evidence of massive ritual fires that once burned for days, consuming horses, hounds, and precious artifacts. It remains a symbolic center of Lithuanian heritage, representing the bridge between temporal power and eternal order.
