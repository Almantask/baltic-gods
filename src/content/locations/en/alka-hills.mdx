The term 'Alka' (or 'Alkas') is foundational to Baltic mythology, designating a sacred space where the community met the divine. These hills and groves, scattered across the Samogitian and Curonian landscapes, were the primary sites for ritual offerings and fire-worship.

An Alka is more than a geographic feature; it is a point of spiritual contact. Whether a hilltop overlooking a river or a secluded grove of ancient oaks, these sites served as the natural cathedrals of the Baltic people, where the laws of nature and the presence of the gods were most keenly felt.
