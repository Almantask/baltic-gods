The forests of Pomesania are home to silent arrangements of stone—sacrificial altars and ritual circles that have outlasted the tribes that built them. These sites were often situated near deep groundwater springs, which the Pomesanians viewed as portals to the spirits.

The stones are the physical remnants of a decentralized but widespread faith. In these clearings, the veil between the world of the living and the realm governed by Velnias was said to be thinnest, allowing for communication with the ancestors and the grounding forces of the earth.
