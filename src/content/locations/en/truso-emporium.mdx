Truso was a legendary early medieval emporium, a vibrant hub of international trade situated on the Vistula Lagoon. Described by the traveler Wulfstan in the 9th century, it was a place where amber was exchanged for the luxuries of the North and the East.

The town was a melting pot of cultures, overseen by the Pomesanians. Its significance was tied to the sea-god Autrimpas, whose favor was sought by the merchants who braved the Baltic waters. Truso's eventually disappearance beneath the shifting sands only added to its mythic status as a lost golden age of Baltic commerce.
