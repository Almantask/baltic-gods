The Imbarė hill fort was one of the major economic and ritual centers of the Curonian Ceklis land. Known for its sophisticated defensive and ritual infrastructure, Imbarė once rivaled the power of other regional strongholds such as Apuolė.

Its position allowed the Curonians to dominate the trade routes between the Baltic sea and the inland regions. Imbarė served as a point of both political sovereignty and spiritual authority, protected by the thunder-god Perkūnas and the ancestral spirits.
