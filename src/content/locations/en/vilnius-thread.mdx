Threads tied above the old loom are left to hang over the stairwell. When a draft sways them toward the door, a traveler is considered safe to depart; if they sway inward, plans are postponed and names are rewoven into the cloth.

Midwives braid thin ribbons here and tuck them into their belts. They say the attic keeps their hands steady, even when storms make the rafters creak.***
