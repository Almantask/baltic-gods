Āraiši is a unique archaeological treasure—a 9th-century lake fortress built on wooden platforms. This settlement allowed the Latgalian inhabitants to live in a liminal space, balanced between the safety of the land and the depth of the water.

The lake dwelling was more than a defensive structure; it was a ritualized landscape where every daily action negotiated the boundary between the human and the spirit worlds. It honors the fluid power of the water-gods and the ingenuity of the Baltic ancestors who thrived in a landscape of mist and wood.
