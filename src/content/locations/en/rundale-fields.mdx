Across the vast Zemgale plains near Rundale, the sky belongs to Pūķis. Farmers watch the night horizons for a streak of fire resembling a dragon, a sign that the household spirit is bringing stolen grain or silver to its master.

During the harvest festivals, special bowls of porridge are left on the thresholds of the granaries. A successful harvest is proof of a well-tended Pūķis, though the fire-breathing spirit demands constant vigilance and respect.
