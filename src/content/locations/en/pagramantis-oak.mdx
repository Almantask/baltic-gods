The Pagramantis oak stands as one of the last living links to the ancient Baltic tradition of tree-worship. Since pre-Christian times, such oaks have been revered as the residence of Perkūnas, the high god of the sky and lightning.

The oak is seen as a living temple, its roots connecting the under-realms and its crown reaching into the heavens. It is a site of collective strength and wisdom, where the community seeks to align their path with the cosmic order of the Baltic spirit.
