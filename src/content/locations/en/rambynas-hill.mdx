Rambynas Hill rises steeply above the Nemunas river, once home to the most famous sacrificial stone in the Baltic lands. Legend says that as long as the stone remained whole, the prosperity of the Scalvian people was guaranteed.

The hill has survived centuries of conflict and change, remaining a site of pilgrimage for those who seek the blessing of Perkūnas. On Midsummer night, fires still glow on its slopes, echoing the ancient tradition of honoring the earth's fertility.
