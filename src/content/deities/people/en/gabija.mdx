## Guardianship of Coals

Gabija is tended each night with whispered thanks. Embers are banked with rye husk and watched until the last sleeper closes their eyes. To offend her by spilling water on a live coal is to invite a cold year and quarrelsome walls.

## Shared Flame

When storms threaten, nearby houses gather their coals into a clay jar and carry them to the kiln shrine. The relighting ceremony at dawn is both practical and sacred, a renewal of household peace under Gabija's gaze.
