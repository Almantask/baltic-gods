## The Giantess Who Built the Shore

Neringa is one of the most beloved figures in Lithuanian coastal folklore: a benevolent giantess whose myth explains the creation of the Curonian Spit. According to legend, the sea dragon Naglis terrorised fishermen, swallowing their boats and churning the waters until no one dared sail. Neringa, a girl of extraordinary size and strength, took pity on them and decided to act.

## Building the Spit

Neringa carried sand from the seabed in her apron, load by load, and poured it out in a long line across the water, building up the peninsula that shields the lagoon from the open sea. The act is one of pure generosity: she creates nothing for herself and asks nothing in return, building a shelter that will protect fishermen for all generations to come.

## Enduring Presence

The Curonian Spit carries her name in modern Lithuanian as Neringa, and the municipality covers the Lithuanian portion today. Her legend is deeply connected to amber, which the sea casts up on the Curonian shore. Folk tradition holds that when the sea is rough and amber is plentiful, Neringa is walking the water's edge again. Fishermen left offerings of amber and ale poured into the sand for her.
