## The Soft Swarm

Austėja moves like a low chorus. Beekeepers call her before lifting a hive, brushing fingers with wax to earn gentle bees. They say every comb built under her gaze gleams darker, holding storms at bay as long as it lasts.

## Pollinated Paths

Along the forest edges, children tie dandelions in their belts to share her favor. If a traveler hears a single bee in winter, it is taken as a sign that Austėja guides them toward shelter. Meadows she blesses bloom unevenly, forming visible paths toward springs and safe fires.
