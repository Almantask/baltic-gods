## Healer of the Old Prussians

Auschauts is the Old Prussian deity of health and healing, listed in the Sudovian Book among the spirits that the Prussians invoked for recovery from illness. Unlike the great cosmological gods of the Romowe triad, Auschauts was a practical deity of daily life, called upon when a child fell sick, when a horse went lame, or when the winter cough settled into a household. He stands in the tradition of healing deities found across the Baltic world.

## Medicine and Ritual

His worship was connected to the use of medicinal herbs, which in Prussian tradition were gathered at specific times and offered before use to secure the deity's blessing. Healers who worked with plant remedies were understood to be drawing on his power, and their knowledge was partly spiritual and partly botanical. Water from sacred springs was a key medium of his healing; springs near Prussian cult sites were associated with treatments for eye ailments and fevers.

## Livestock and Human Health Together

In Baltic agricultural economies, the boundaries between human and animal health were closely intertwined. Auschauts was invoked simultaneously for sick people and sick livestock, reflecting a holistic view of household welfare. The recovery of a valued horse or cow was as much a matter of ritual as medicine, and healing ceremonies for animals could involve offerings of grain, milk, and carved wooden charms.
