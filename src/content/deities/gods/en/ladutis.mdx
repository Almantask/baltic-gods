## Lado / Ladutis — The Divine Exclamation of Harmonious Joy

Of all the refrain words in Baltic song tradition, *Lado* is the most widespread and the most structurally central. It appears in Lithuanian spring songs, Latvian midsummer chants, Ukrainian ritual music, and Polish folk singing. Whether this indicates a shared proto-Slavic and proto-Baltic deity, an ancient word meaning 'harmony' or 'fitting,' or simply a deeply satisfying sound that human voices in celebration produce — scholars have debated for two centuries without resolution. What is undeniable is its emotional truth: when a group of people singing together shout *Lado!*, something precise and joyful happens in the room.

In Lithuanian singing tradition, *lado* and its variant *ladutė* appear most prominently in spring fertility songs and in the *sutartinės* of Aukštaitija. Greimas proposed that *lado* derived from the same PIE root as *lad-* (to fit, to order, to place correctly), making Lado a deity of harmonic arrangement — the principle that things should fit together well. The *ladutis* form (with the diminutive *-utis*) suggests endearment, as if one were addressing not a great cosmic force but a beloved presence, a small and intimate god of small and intimate happinesses: the bread that rises perfectly, the couple that suits each other, the harvest song sung in tune.

In Latvian tradition, parallel forms (*Lālo, Lado*) appear in summer solstice contexts, particularly in the Jāņi (Midsummer) celebration. Latvian folklore scholars including Krišjānis Barons documented hundreds of *dainas* (traditional songs) containing these refrains, treating them as survivals of an ancient religious practice in which singing specific syllables at specific seasons maintained the world's proper operation. Whether Lado was ever a named goddess with a mythology or was always an abstract principle given momentary personal form by the act of singing — the distinction may not have mattered to those who sang. The sound itself was the devotion.
