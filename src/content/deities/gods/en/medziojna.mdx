## Medziojna — Goddess of the Hunt

Medziojna is the Lithuanian goddess of the hunt, recorded by Jan Łasicki in the late sixteenth century among the deities venerated in Samogitia. Her name derives from *medžioti*, to hunt, and she presided over the success of every chase. Hunters invoked her before setting out into the forest, asking her blessing for swift pursuit and a fortunate outcome.

She is closely associated with Žvoruna and is sometimes treated as an alternate name for the same divine force. Whereas Žvoruna emphasizes sovereignty over wild beasts, Medziojna emphasizes the act of hunting itself — the strategy, endurance, and fortune required to bring game home. She rewarded hunters who showed reverence and punished those who were wasteful or cruel.

Offerings to Medziojna were made at the edge of the forest before a hunt, typically a portion of bread or the first blood of any game taken. Sacred trees at the forest boundary served as her altars, where hunters whispered prayers and left tokens in her honor. Her presence was felt most keenly at dawn, when the boundary between the human world and the wild was thinnest.

In later folklore Medziojna merged with the figure of the forest mother, a composite goddess who both granted and withdrew game from human reach. Her legacy persists in the Lithuanian word for hunter, *medžiotojas*, and in the seasonal rituals of forest communities who still acknowledge the sacred compact between hunter and hunted.
