## Lord of the Solstice Night

Janis is the Latvian deity of midsummer, embodying the longest day and shortest night of the year. His festival, Jani (celebrated on the evening of June 23 and through June 24), draws the entire Latvian people outdoors. Farmsteads are decorated with oak and fern branches, cattle are garlanded with flowers, and bonfires are lit on every hillside so that their light is visible across the landscape.

## The Songs of Jani

The dainas (folk songs) associated with Jani number in the thousands and form one of the richest bodies of seasonal song in Europe. Gathered in Krisjani Barons's monumental six-volume collection, they describe Janis riding on horseback through the countryside, checking that every farm has green branches at the gate and every family has prepared the Midsummer cheese (Janu siers). They address him personally, bargaining and celebrating.

## Fire, Dew, and the Fern Blossom

Midsummer night carries its own sacred calendar of acts. Young couples leap over the Jani bonfire together for luck and fertility. Unmarried women bathe their faces in the morning dew for beauty and fortune. The fern blossom, said to bloom only at this moment, is sought in the deep forest by those brave enough to enter alone, promising treasure or wisdom to whoever finds it. Janis's presence is felt through the night in the warmth of fire, the smell of smoke and clover, and the sound of songs carried on the short summer dark.
