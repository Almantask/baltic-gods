## Miglas māte — Do Not Follow the Mist

Latvian folk belief is precise about the fog: it is not simply low cloud, not simply bad visibility, not simply a weather condition to wait out. The fog is Miglas māte's domain, and her domain is the liminal space — the place where normal rules of geography and identity stop applying. The forest path you know perfectly well in clear weather becomes a different path in her mist. You might follow it for an hour and find yourself in a place you have never seen. The fog does not merely hide things; it moves things.

The boundary-blurring quality of mist made Miglas māte specifically connected to the membrane between the living and the dead. In the Baltic cosmological scheme, the world of the living and the world of Veļi (the dead) are not located in different places; they are located in the same place but in different registers. The forest, the bog, the misty river valley — these are where the registers overlap. Miglas māte governed those overlapping zones, which is why her mist was dangerous: in her territory, you might cross into the dead's register without intending to, and then be unable to find your way back.

Šmits documented the folk advice: when caught in fog, stop walking. Light a fire if possible. Call out to family names. Do not follow any sound or any shape that seems to beckon. Miglas māte herself does not intend harm; she is not an aggressive spirit. But her domain is indifferent to human navigation, and its disorienting properties are absolute. The appropriate response is stillness and patience, not pursuit. Wait for the mist to lift on its own terms, and do not mistake its temporary voids for paths to follow.
