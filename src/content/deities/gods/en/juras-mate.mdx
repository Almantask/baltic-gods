## Juras mate — Mother of the Sea

Juras mate, the Mother of the Sea, is one of the most powerful of the Latvian Mate spirits — the great chorus of 'Mother' deities who govern every domain of the natural world. She is the sovereign of the Baltic Sea, commanding its depth, its waves, and all the life that swims within it from her amber palace on the sea floor.

The Latvian dainas portray Juras mate as a complex figure. She is both the ultimate provider and a force that demands respect. Fishermen of the Latvian coast — particularly in Kurzeme and Vidzeme — cast offerings into the sea before setting out: the first catch of the morning, a portion of bread, or a few drops of beer. These gifts acknowledged her power and implicitly asked for permission to fish her domain.

Juras mate's amber palace is one of the most evocative images in Baltic mythology. Amber — found washed up on Baltic shores for millennia — was understood as the gift or the tears of the sea. In some traditions, amber was said to be pieces of the sun that fell into the sea when Saule descended at dusk. The sea-floor palace creates a world beneath the waves that mirrors the world above, a parallel cosmos ruled by feminine sovereign power.

The Mate complex as a whole reflects a theological system unique to Latvian mythology: a vast network of specific female powers governing every element of the world, from fire (Uguns mate) to wind (Veja mate) to the forest (Meza mate). Juras mate stands at the most dramatic edge of this feminine cosmos — the horizon where sky meets sea.
