## Queen of the Shades

Velu mate is the Latvian ruler of the underworld, the great feminine power who receives every soul that dies and guides the shades of the departed in the realm below the living world. In Latvian folk belief the dead are not utterly gone: they return during Velu laiks (the Time of the Shades, around late October and early November), entering homes as honoured guests to sit at the table and share a meal with the living.

## The Underworld and Ancestor Memory

Velu mate's realm is not conceived as a place of punishment but as a continuation. The dead live on, tending their own fields, keeping their own fires, but invisible and beyond the horizon of the living. Their welfare is tied to the memory and offerings of those they left behind: uneaten food goes to them, unlit candles grieve them, forgotten names diminish them.

## Offerings and Remembrance

During Velu laiks, Latvian families set extra places at the table, left food and drink for the returning dead, and kept candles burning through the night. Birch-bark bowls with grain and apple were floated on springs and streams near graves. Prayers addressed to Velu mate asked her to convey messages to named ancestors: that they are remembered, that the harvest was good, that a child was born.
