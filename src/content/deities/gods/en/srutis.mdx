## Srutis — Spirit of the River Current

Srutis is the Samogitian spirit of the river current, distinct from Upinis the general river-lord. Recorded by Jan Łasicki in *De Diis Samagitarum* as *Srutim pro flumine*, Srutis embodies the specific force of moving water — the current itself, its speed, and its power to carry or resist.

Mill operators, ferrymen, and those who built bridges over Lithuanian rivers propitiated Srutis with offerings placed in the current: bread, coins, or small woven wreaths released upstream. The belief was that the current had its own will, capable of blessing or frustrating human work along the waterway.

In the hierarchy of Baltic water spirits, Srutis represents a specialized function: not the lordship of a named river, but the animating force common to all flowing water. His domain was the boundary between stillness and motion, the living edge where the river exerts its independent agency.
