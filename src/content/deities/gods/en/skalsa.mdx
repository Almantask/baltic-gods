## Skalsa — Spirit of Household Abundance

Skalsa is the Samogitian spirit of domestic prosperity and fullness, recorded by Jan Łasicki as *Skalsam pro abundantia domestica* — invoked for the abundance of the household. She presides over the granary, the storehouse, and the table — ensuring that provisions remain ample and that the household's needs are met through each season.

Offerings to Skalsa were made at harvest time when grain and preserved foods were brought indoors. A portion of the first loaves baked from new flour, or a dish of the first pickled vegetables, would be set aside at the hearth as a gesture of acknowledgment and gratitude. Her blessing was believed to "stretch" the household stores, making them last longer than expected.

Skalsa belongs to the category of Lithuanian household spirits who administered the daily economy of the farm. Her presence ensured not merely sufficiency but a generosity that allowed the family to host guests and fulfill obligations of hospitality — a central virtue in Baltic social life.
