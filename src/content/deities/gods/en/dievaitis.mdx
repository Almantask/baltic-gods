## Dievaitis / Dieviņš — The Little God

Dievaitis (Lithuanian) and Dieviņš (Latvian) are the diminutive, approachable aspects of the supreme sky god Dievas. While Dievas is often depicted as a distant, majestic celestial figure, the Dievaitis is the same divinity in a form that is close, tender, and accessible to the everyday farmer. In folk songs and prayers, the Little God is addressed with the intimacy reserved for a trusted elder — a blacksmith who helps repair the plowshare, a rider who checks on the fields at dawn.

Algirdas Julius Greimas analyzed the Dievaitis as a mediating figure: the bridge between transcendent divinity and the human need for a responsive, present god. In many folk lyric contexts, the Dievaitis is interchangeable with Perkūnas in his youthful form — the aspect that acts in the human world rather than presiding from above. He arrives on a white horse, dismounts at the field boundary, and blesses the soil with his touch.

In Latvian dainas, the Dieviņš is one of the most beloved figures in the entire folk corpus. He is fair, generous to the deserving, and particularly protective of orphans and widows. His name is spoken not with fear or awe but with affection, reflecting the Baltic understanding that the divine is not only powerful but genuinely interested in human flourishing.
