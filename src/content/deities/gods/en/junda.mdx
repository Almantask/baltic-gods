## Junda

Junda is the Lithuanian goddess of war and victory, whose name is linked to the concepts of struggle, uprising, and the fierce determination of the warrior spirit (*judeti* / *jundeti*). She represents the divine force that drives combatants forward in defense of their land and kin.

In the Baltic worldview, warfare was not merely a political act but a sacred undertaking. Before a campaign, warriors offered prayers and sacrifices to ensure divine favor. Junda embodied the courage that transforms fear into action — the inner flame that could not be extinguished by doubt or hardship. She was invoked to steel the hearts of warriors and to guide the outcome of battle toward a just victory.

Historical scholars note that Junda's prominence rose significantly in the 19th century, when Teodor Narbutt and other Romantic historians sought to document a complete Baltic pantheon modeled on the Greco-Roman tradition. Norbertas Velius and later researchers have questioned the depth of her pre-Christian cult, suggesting that while the concept of a war deity was authentic, her specific persona may have been shaped by reconstructive zeal as much as primary sources.

Despite scholarly debate, Junda resonates as a symbol of ancestral bravery. She represents the spirit of those who defended Baltic lands through centuries of crusades, invasions, and resistance — a divine presence who witnessed the ancient martial struggles of her people and honored those who fought with honor.
