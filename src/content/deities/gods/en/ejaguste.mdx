## Ejagustė — Spirit of Hunting Fortune

Ejagustė is the Lithuanian spirit of hunting luck, listed by Łasicki among the numerous spirits who governed daily life and livelihood in Samogitia. The name appears to combine *ėjimas* (going, movement) with *gusti* (to sense fortune), suggesting a spirit who accompanies the hunter in motion and guides luck along the trail.

Unlike Medziojna, Ejagustė governs not the whole domain of hunting but fortune within the chase itself — the lucky angle of light that reveals a deer’s tracks, the sudden stillness that allows a clean shot, the instinct that tells a hunter when to wait and when to advance. She was not worshipped with elaborate ritual but acknowledged with small signs: a whispered word of thanks, a moment of stillness before the hunt began.

Scholars associate Ejagustė with a broader class of Lithuanian fortune spirits who governed specific moments of activity rather than entire domains. She belongs to the same tradition as spirits overseeing the fortune of travelers, fishermen, and craftsmen — each one a divine companion for a particular kind of luck.

A hunter suffering persistent bad luck might resort to special offerings at a crossroads or under a prominent forest tree, asking Ejagustė to return and walk with them once more. Her absence was felt as misfortune: a fruitless day in the forest, game that vanished without trace, traps that always came up empty.
