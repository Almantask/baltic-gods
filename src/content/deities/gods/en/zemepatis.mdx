## Zemepatis — Lord of the Homestead

Zemepatis, 'the lord of the earth,' is the divine master of the domesticated landscape. Where Zemyna represents the wild, fertile earth in its entirety, Zemepatis governs the human-cultivated space: the plowed field, the vegetable garden, the stable, and the orchard. He is the guardian of boundaries and the protector of all that the farmer has built and tended.

Historical sources beginning with Martynas Mazyvdas's 1547 catechism record Zemepatis among the named deities to whom Lithuanian peasants still made offerings at night. Jan Lasicki's more detailed 16th-century account confirms him as a major household deity invoked specifically for the protection of property against fire, pests, and misfortune.

Zemepatis is closely associated with the zaltys — the sacred grass snake that was kept beneath the hearth or in the stable as the physical manifestation of the home's vital spirit. A zaltys living in the household was considered Zemepatis's earthly representative. Killing one was a serious sacrilege that would bring ruin to the family.

Ritually, offerings of bread, beer, and first-fruits were made at the field borders to honor Zemepatis at the turning points of the agricultural year. The spring plowing and autumn harvest were the most important times to seek his favor, ensuring that the soil remained productive and the household safe.
