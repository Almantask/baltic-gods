## Linksmine

Linksmine is the Lithuanian goddess of happiness, laughter, and social merriment. Her name is a direct derivation of *linksmas* (joyful) and *linksmintis* (to rejoice). Recorded by Jan Lasicki in his catalogue of Samogitian gods as a deity invoked *pro gaudio* — for the sake of joy — she represents the divine blessing of a light heart and a house full of warmth.

In the Baltic world, genuine happiness was understood as a gift from the divine — not merely a product of circumstances, but a state of grace conferred by a benevolent spiritual force. The family that laughed together, sang together, and feasted together was a family under divine protection. Linksmine was the guardian of that sacred atmosphere, the invisible presence who turned a gathering of people into a true community.

She was invoked at weddings, where joy was essential to a good beginning; at harvest festivals, where the abundance of the earth was celebrated with song and drink; and at communal *talka* work-gatherings, where the labor of the fields was lightened by camaraderie. Her blessing was sought especially at the start of these events, when a single cold or discordant note might cast a shadow over the whole occasion.

Linksmine reminds us that the Baltic people did not separate the sacred from the social. Every feast was a ritual, every shared song an offering, and every genuine burst of laughter a moment in which the gods came close to their worshippers.
