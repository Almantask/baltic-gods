## Rasa

Rasa is the Baltic goddess of morning dew and the freshness of the dawn. Her name is the Lithuanian and Latvian word for dew (*rasa*), and she is understood to be a daughter or handmaid of the Sun goddess Saule, entrusted with the daily task of washing the face of the world before the sun rises fully into the sky.

In Baltic folk belief, the dew that appears on grass, flowers, and leaves in the early morning was not mere condensation but a sacred, life-giving substance — the tears or the cleansing water of the celestial realm, fallen to bless the earth. The woman who washed her face in the first dew of midsummer was believed to gain beauty and health; the farmer who walked his fields at dawn in the dew was bringing himself into alignment with the divine order of nature.

Rasa's name is also enshrined in the major midsummer festival — *Rasu svente* (Dew Festival / Midsummer Eve), one of the most important celebrations in the Lithuanian and Latvian calendars. On the eve of the summer solstice, bonfires were lit, ferns were sought, and the dew itself was harvested in linen cloths and bowls, then applied to the skin or the crops as a purifying and fertilizing medicine.

Krišjanis Barons catalogued numerous Latvian *dainas* (songs) in which the dew is treated as a sacred, personified substance — a gift of the Sun's daughters to the waiting earth. Rasa represents the transient yet essential purity of the natural world: here for only a moment each morning, yet returning faithfully with every dawn.
