## Zvoruna — Mistress of Wild Beasts

Zvoruna is one of the most ancient and fierce goddesses of the Baltic pantheon. While she is often identified with Medeina as a goddess of the hunt, her name draws from the word zveris — wild beast — and emphasizes her sovereignty specifically over the animals of the forest rather than the forest itself.

Historically attested in the Hypatian Codex and Dlugosz's chronicles, Zvoruna was revered especially by hunters. She demanded respect: those who hunted without necessity or showed cruelty to animals were punished. She is typically depicted as a fierce young huntress accompanied by a pack of wolves, her most sacred animals.

The distinction between Zvoruna and Medeine in scholarship remains debated. Some scholars see Zvoruna as a Samogitian regional variant; others interpret her as a specialized aspect emphasizing animal governance with the wolf, hare, and bear all under her domain.

For Baltic hunters, no game was taken carelessly. The first kill of the season required an offering: a portion of the meat or the first blood poured on tree roots in her honor. Failure to do so invited calamity on the next hunt.
