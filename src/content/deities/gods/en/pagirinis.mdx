# Pagirinis — The Sacred Snake Beneath the Quern

In the heart of every Lithuanian home, beneath the grinding stone where grain was turned to flour, there lived a spirit of immense importance: **Pagirinis**, the sacred grass snake. His very name derived from *pagirnis* — the space beneath the quern — marking it as the most guarded threshold of the household. Pagirinis was not merely a symbol; in most families, a real grass snake (*Žaltys*) was kept, fed bowls of milk, and treated with the deepest reverence.

The cult of Pagirinis belongs to the oldest stratum of Baltic belief, predating even the historical gods of the pantheons recorded by 16th-century chroniclers. Jan Łasicki noted the deity in his list of Samogitian spirits, describing offerings of milk given to the guardian spirit beneath the grinding stone. To kill a grass snake — especially one found near the hearth — was among the gravest sins a family could commit. It was said that such an act would invite lightning strikes upon the house, the death of cattle, or the sudden passing of children.

Pagirinis stood apart from other domestic spirits. While **Dimstipatis** watched over the farmstead as a whole and **Lauksargis** guarded the fields, Pagirinis presided over the innermost sanctum of the home and the intimate bond between the living family and its ancestral dead. The snake, coiling beneath the stones, was understood as a conduit between the world above and the *mirusiųjų pasaulis* — the realm of the departed. His presence ensured that the family's *laimė* (luck and fate) remained intact and that the sacred fire of Gabija continued to burn without interruption.

During major feast days, especially the night of the summer solstice and the autumn harvest, the family would place a fresh bowl of milk near the quern and speak words of gratitude to Pagirinis. In return, he watched over the children as they slept, kept rodents from the grain stores, and silently absorbed the prayers of the ancestors into the living earth beneath the floor. His coiled form, resting in the cool darkness, was the living seal of the family's covenant with the gods.
