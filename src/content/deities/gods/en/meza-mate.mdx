## Mother of the Living Forest

Meza mate is among the most beloved of the Latvian mate (mother) spirits, a vast category of divine female presences that governs every domain of the natural world. She is the personified guardian spirit of all forests, the mother of every creature living among the trees: wolves, bears, deer, owls, and serpents. Where humans enter her realm, they enter under her watch.

## Asking Permission

Traditional Latvian forest practice was governed by a fundamental obligation: permission must be sought before any act of taking. A woodcutter about to fell a tree would address the oldest tree nearby, acknowledging Meza mate's presence and asking forgiveness. A hunter entering forest would tell her what he intended to hunt and why, promising not to take more than was needed. Offerings left at hollow lindens and ancient oaks, bread, salt, and the first mushrooms of the season, were her due.

## The Forest as Living Body

In the Latvian cosmological imagination, the forest is not an environment but a living being, and Meza mate is its consciousness. The crackling of branches on a still day, the sudden hush when all birdsong stops, the inexplicable sense of being watched in deep timber: all these were her signs. She appears in dainas as a tall grey-robed woman who moves between trees without touching the ground, her hair braided with fern and moss.
