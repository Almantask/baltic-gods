## Veja mate — Mother of the Wind

Veja mate, the Mother of the Wind, governs the air and the four cardinal winds of the Latvian mythological world. She is one of the most active and vivid of the Latvian Mate deities — impossible to ignore and impossible to control, a force present in every shaken tree, every filled sail, every grain scattered by the wind on the threshing floor.

In the Latvian dainas, Veja mate is often described as an elderly woman with wild, streaming hair who strides through the forest, shaking treetops and driving rain before her. She is invoked by sailors to fill their sails for a safe homeward voyage, and by farmers to winnow chaff from grain in the autumn harvest. Her breath is vital: without wind, the grain could not be separated from the husk.

Veja mate's power is inherently dual. The same wind that fills a sail can capsize a boat. The same breeze that winnows grain can fan a fire into destruction. Baltic farmers and sailors understood this duality intimately — and their ritual relationship with Veja mate was accordingly one of cautious respect rather than simple petition.

In some Latvian traditions, Veja mate is said to be the mother of the four Wind Sons, each governing one cardinal direction. This makes her not just a governing force but a progenitor — the source of all directional movement in the atmosphere. Her position in the Mate hierarchy reflects the central importance of wind in a culture that depended on it for fishing, navigation, grain processing, and the great autumn storms that signaled the end of the harvest season.
