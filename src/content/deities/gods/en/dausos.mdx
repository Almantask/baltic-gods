## Dausos — The Luminous Afterlife

Dausos is the Lithuanian personification of the afterlife paradise — a luminous realm located at the summit of a glass or silver mountain where the spirits of the dead (*vėlės*) find eternal rest in a land of perpetual spring. Unlike the darker underworld domain of Velnias, Dausos represents the cosmic "above" where deserving souls achieve a state of peaceful light.

The journey to Dausos was imagined as a steep and difficult climb. In archaic burial practice, Lithuanian and Old Prussian graves included the claws of lynxes or bears, believed to help the soul grip the glass surface of the mountain as it ascended. The smoke of the funeral pyre was thought to carry the soul upward, guided by the ancestral spirits already residing in Dausos.

Gintaras Beresňevičius, in his foundational study *Dausos* (1990), analyzed the concept as a mountain-paradise that reflects the Proto-Baltic theology of divine light. The souls residing in Dausos continued to watch over their living descendants, making Dausos inseparable from the ancestor cult that formed the spiritual backbone of Baltic community life.
