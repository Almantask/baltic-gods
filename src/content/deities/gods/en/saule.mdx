## Radiant Weave

Saulė is sung as a mother who spins dawn from flax and gold. The looms of Žemaitija carry her sigil, and every thread is believed to hold a mile of light. At Midsummer, families lift mirrors toward the rising disk to pull good harvest heat into their homes.

## Heat as Blessing

Where she pauses, marsh water steams and berries ripen twice. Children chase the first rays across dunes, telling that the sun hides amber tears where the tide cannot reach. In winter, her absence is mourned with bells buried in snow and unearthed at the first thaw.
