## Negali — The God of Impossibility

Negali, whose name literally means "Cannot," is the Lithuanian personification of cosmic limits and impossibility. Recorded by Jan Długosz in the 15th century among the gods of the Lithuanians, Negali represents the divine enforcement of boundaries — the point at which human will, however determined, cannot proceed because fate and cosmic law forbid it.

In Baltic ethics, acknowledging Negali was an act of spiritual maturity. To invoke Negali was to accept that certain outcomes lay outside the human domain regardless of effort or prayer. He was not a punishing figure but a structural one: the boundary between the possible and the impossible that maintains the hierarchy of fate (*Dalia*) and the authority of the gods. Hubris — the attempt to exceed one's allotted measure — was precisely what Negali guarded against.

Modern Baltic scholars interpret Negali as the personified negative aspect of fate: where Laima represents what will be granted, Negali represents what will be withheld. Together they form the complete structure of destiny, ensuring that neither excessive hope nor excessive despair distorts a person's orientation toward life's actual possibilities.
